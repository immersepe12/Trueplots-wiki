# TruePlots — Go-to-Market Plan v3.0

*Supersedes GTM v2.0. Key changes:*
- *Month-4 decision restructured from single greenlight to three-gate check (farmland pilots + RERA memo + urban supply validation)*
- *Urban fractional deal target compressed: 20+ urban deals with 10+ fractional by month 9 (not month 12) due to NoBroker fast-follow threat*
- *A-Khata-only rule at urban launch; B-Khata plots excluded (financing constraint breaks fractional affordability)*
- *Urban Site Ops Lead hiring pulled forward from month 7 to month 4–5 (non-optional if urban launches)*
- *NoBroker as primary urban threat; defensibility window tightened 9–12 months (not 18)*

---

## 1. The Starting Position

TruePlots launches past the cold-start problem. Four months of founder field work has produced 50+ verified farmland parcels across Mandya, Maddur, Ramanagara. Delight Eco Farms adds farmland legal ops, subdivision expertise, sub-registrar relationships, plus BBMP/BDA-side experience from their layout development work that enables urban sites as a second vertical.

The 5000+ warm founder network is a high-affinity buyer pool for the critical commercial risk (stranger-pool CAC in fractional deals).

**Sequencing:** farmland full-throttle from day 1. Urban sites gated through three checks at month 4. If all three pass, urban launches months 4–6. If any fails, urban delays to month 6–9 and re-gates.

## 2. The 12-Month Goal

- **30+ fractional farmland deals** closed cleanly (moat-flip threshold)
- **20+ whole-plot farmland deals** closed
- **20+ urban site deals with 10+ fractional by month 9** (compressed from month 12 due to NoBroker threat)
- Operational templates locked across both categories
- Top 3 Google rankings on 5+ farmland queries, 3+ urban site queries
- Ready for Tamil Nadu farmland expansion and multi-city urban expansion in year 2

## 3. Strategic Framework

### 3.1 Positioning

Farmland-first hero, urban sites as secondary category.

Hero: *"Own premium farmland from ₹40 lakh."*
Sub-hero: *"Verified plots across South India. Farmland and urban sites. Individually titled. Legally clear."*

### 3.2 Sequencing Principle

**Farmland full-throttle from day 1. Urban sites gated month 4.** Reasons:

- Farmland supply is ready; urban supply needs sourcing
- Farmland moat is clearer; urban sites face NoBroker fast-follow
- Farmland operational model needs validation before dual-track attention
- RERA classification must be resolved before urban transactions

### 3.3 Competitive Compression

Defensibility windows differ:

- **Farmland:** 12–18 months uncontested. Primary threats (Hasiru, Farmland Bazaar) are slower.
- **Urban sites:** 9–12 months uncontested. NoBroker has $300M+ raised, 6,768 Bangalore plot listings, paid verification upsell live, and product velocity that could replicate TruePlots' offering in 1–2 quarters.

**Urban sites moat cannot be the marketplace UI** — that's copyable in a sprint. The moat must be the operational stack: BBMP khata bifurcation playbook, licensed surveyor network, CIS-proof legal templates, deal execution SOPs. Execute 20+ urban fractional deals by month 9 or forfeit the category.

## 5. Phase 1: Farmland Validation (Months 1–4)

**Goal:** 10 deals closed (5 whole-plot, 3–5 fractional). Affinity CAC validated. Three-gate decision at end of month 4.

### Month 1 — Activation

- Platform live with 15 farmland listings
- Personal broadcast to top 500 network contacts
- First 5 site visits, founder-led
- First fractional plot activated (7-acre Mandya pitched to 4–5 warm buyers)
- 2 whole-plot deals advancing
- **New:** RERA counsel engaged; memo drafting begins

### Month 2 — First Closes

- 2 whole-plot farmland deals close
- First fractional deal activates (3 buyers committed, Delight acquires)
- Second broadcast wave with social proof
- Founder LinkedIn Diary begins
- **New:** RERA memo draft received and reviewed

### Month 3 — Referral Activation

- First buyer case studies live
- Referral program launched (₹25K per closed referral)
- Delight farmland customer base receives first-look offering
- Third broadcast wave
- **New:** Urban supply mystery-shop begins (Gate 3 input)
- **New:** RERA memo finalized

### Month 4 — Three-Gate Decision

**Target:** 10 deals closed cumulative. All three gates evaluated.

**Gate 1 — Farmland pilot validation:**
- ≥6 of 10 deals had majority-affinity acquisition
- First 5 farmland fractional deals closed cleanly (no subdivision defects, no title issues surfaced)
- Fractional buyer CAC <₹25K

**Gate 2 — RERA classification confirmed:**
- Written memo from Karnataka RERA specialist confirming TruePlots as facilitator, not promoter, under current statute
- Memo covers both farmland and urban site transactions
- Unit economics hold under the confirmed classification

**Gate 3 — Urban supply density validated:**
- Mystery-shop confirms >100 A-Khata plots per month in ₹60–80L range across Devanahalli, Sarjapur, Whitefield periphery, Kanakapura Road, Bannerghatta
- 3+ BMRDA layout developers open to supply partnership for stuck 60×40 inventory

**Decision tree:**

- **All three pass:** urban sites launch Phase 2, month 4–6. Hire Urban Site Ops Lead immediately.
- **Any gate fails:** urban launch delays to month 6–9. Farmland focus continues. Re-gate at month 6.
- **Farmland Gate 1 fails (red on affinity):** fractional product redesign required before any urban expansion considered.

## 10. Risks and Decision Gates

### 10.1 Top Risks

1. **NoBroker fast-follow on urban sites** — 1–2 quarter replication risk
2. **RERA facilitator-vs-promoter classification unresolved** — blocks clean urban launch
3. **79A/B restoration** — farmland TAM collapse
4. **Vertical sprawl** — three-gate sequencing mitigates
5. **Affinity channel burnout** — disciplined outreach required
6. **First fractional deal failure** — over-invest in first 3 deals
7. **B-Khata exclusion narrows urban TAM** — mitigation: A-Khata-only at launch
8. **Founder bandwidth** — Urban Ops Lead by month 4–5 non-negotiable

### 10.2 Decision Gates

- **Month 4:** three-gate evaluation (farmland pilots + RERA memo + urban supply). If all green, urban launches. If any fails, urban delays to month 6–9.
- **Month 6:** paid acquisition CAC gate. Scale if <₹30K per deal.
- **Month 6 (if urban delayed):** three-gate re-evaluation for urban launch.
- **Month 9:** urban moat-flip checkpoint. 20+ urban deals, 10+ fractional? If not, reassess urban viability against NoBroker position.
- **Month 12:** farmland moat-flip. 30+ fractional farmland deals? If not, reassess.
