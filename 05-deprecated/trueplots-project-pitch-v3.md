# TruePlots

*South India's verified plots marketplace — farmland-first, urban sites alongside, with fractional access to plots you couldn't afford alone.*

---

## What we're building

A marketplace for plots in South India where every listing is legally verified before the buyer sees it, and where plots too large for a single buyer can be jointly purchased with others — with full legal subdivision, easement deeds, and individual titles handled end-to-end.

Farmland is the hero. Four months of sourced supply, the 3-buyer-advance fractional model, and the Delight Eco Farms operational partnership are all purpose-built for farmland. The same verification infrastructure, legal rigor, and Delight's layout-development experience extend naturally to urban site plots — A-Khata 30×40s, 40×60s, and 60×40s across Bangalore peripheral corridors — where the same two problems (legal fraud, price inaccessibility) exist in a different wrapper.

A buyer picks a plot. Three buyers commit advances (two for urban 60×40 → 30×40 splits). Our capital acquires the plot. We handle the legal separation. Each buyer ends up with their own individually-titled slice.

No pooling. No strangers. No coordination risk.

## The problem

Plot buying in Karnataka — farmland or urban site — is the worst high-value purchase experience urban Indians routinely undertake. Two independent problems exist, and no existing player solves both:

**1. Legal and informational fraud.** Discovery is broker-gated. Documentation is unreliable. Farmland buyers get blindsided by PTCL claims, unconverted land, contested ownership, 79A/B restrictions. Urban site buyers get blindsided by disputed A-Khata/B-Khata status, contested layouts, BBMP vs BDA jurisdiction issues, unapproved subdivisions. All of it surfaces *after* a buyer has committed money.

**2. Price inaccessibility.** A ₹1.5Cr 7-acre farmland plot that four ₹40L buyers would split stays unsold. A ₹1.5Cr A-Khata 60×40 urban site that two ₹75L buyers could divide into two 30×40s stays unsold. Same stuck-inventory pattern, different scale. Nobody coordinates the split.

The founder spent four months and two failed farmland deals before deciding to solve this as a business.

## The model

TruePlots operates as three interlocking layers across both plot categories:

1. **Verified marketplace.** Every listing checked — farmland against Bhoomi, KGIS, PTCL, encumbrance, 79A/B status; urban sites against BBMP/BDA records, A-Khata status (B-Khata plots excluded at launch), CDP zoning, approved layout orders. Buyers receive a standardized TruePlots Verified Report with every plot.
2. **Fractional facilitation.** Plots too large for one buyer sold as slices once buyer thresholds are met. Delight's capital acquires the plot. TruePlots handles subdivision and individual titling.
3. **Full-stack services.** Registration, conversion (farmland), khata transfer (urban), construction partnerships.

**Positioning:** *"Verified plots across South India — farmland, urban sites, all individually titled, all legally clear."*

## What makes this hard to copy

**Delight Eco Farms is our parent and equity partner.** Years of farmland subdivision, layout development, sub-registrar work, and BBMP-side experience mean TruePlots launches with in-house legal and operational capability that a standalone startup takes 18 months to build. Plot acquisition capital comes from Delight's balance sheet.

**The 3-buyer-advance structure is cleanly outside regulated territory.** Customer funds are never pooled to acquire assets (the failure mode SEBI cited when shutting Growpital down in 2024). Customers don't face coordination risk. We don't face inventory risk.

**Farmland supply is already in hand.** Four months of founder field work has produced 50+ verified parcels ready to list across Mandya, Maddur, Ramanagara. Urban site supply comes online in months 4–6 through Delight's layout network, Bangalore broker relationships, and BMRDA layout developer partnerships (stuck 60×40 inventory maps directly to the 2-buyer split use case).

**Operational stack, not UI, is the moat.** Marketplace UIs can be copied in a sprint. The defensibility lives in the BBMP khata bifurcation playbook, licensed surveyor network, CIS-proof legal templates, and deal execution SOPs. This takes 9–12 months minimum to build from scratch.

## Honest risks

- **NoBroker fast-follow on urban sites.** They could replicate verification + fractional in 1–2 quarters. Mitigation: 20+ urban fractional deals by month 9, moat in operational stack not UI.
- **Regulatory.** Section 79A/B in Karnataka (farmland) is currently repealed but politically contested. Reversal would collapse ~70% of farmland TAM; we'd accelerate Tamil Nadu and lean harder into urban sites.
- **RERA classification.** Urban plot fractional may fall under RERA if classified as "promoter" rather than "facilitator." We're resolving this via Karnataka RERA specialist counsel memo before any urban launch.
- **Vertical sprawl.** Running farmland and urban sites simultaneously risks fragmenting founder attention. Mitigation: strict three-gate sequencing at month 4; farmland full-throttle until stable.
- **Unit economics.** The assumption that warm-network acquisition reliably delivers committed buyers per fractional deal is validated on pilots but not at scale. Month-4 gate either greenlights scale or forces redesign.
- **Category history.** Most Indian farmland investment startups have failed. Growpital was shut down by SEBI in 2024. We've engineered carefully around that specific failure mode.

## Why now

Two unstoppable forces: urban Indian professionals are approaching plot ownership — farmland and urban — with more seriousness than any prior generation (post-COVID shifts, wealth planning, lifestyle changes), and the supply side has not modernized in 20 years. The first team to own verified + accessible plots in South India will compound for a decade. The window is open now and closes in 9–18 months.
