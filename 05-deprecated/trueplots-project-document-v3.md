## 1. Executive Summary

TruePlots is a verified plots marketplace for South India with built-in fractional access for buyers who cannot afford premium plots alone. Every listing is legally verified before buyers see it. Plots too large for a single buyer are sold as individual slices once three buyers commit, with TruePlots facilitating full legal separation — subdivision, easement deeds, individual titles.

Two plot categories are in scope, sequenced deliberately:

- **Farmland (hero product)** — rural parcels across Karnataka, verified against revenue-department records. Launches month 0. 50+ parcels sourced.
- **Urban site plots (secondary, conditional)** — A-Khata residential plots in Bangalore peripheral corridors, verified against BBMP/BDA records. Launches month 4 *only if* three gates pass.

Farmland leads because the moat is clearest (Delight's rural legal depth, underserved ₹25–60L fractional segment, CIS-proof 3-buyer model, thin competitive field). Urban sites extend addressable market — but compete against a venture-backed fast-mover (NoBroker) with a 9–12 month window to establish operational lead.

**Year-1 goal:** 30+ fractional farmland deals + 20+ whole-plot farmland deals + 20+ urban site deals (of which 10+ fractional) by month 9. The month-9 urban milestone is compressed from earlier planning due to NoBroker fast-follow risk.

## 2. The Problem

### 2.1 Legal and Informational Fraud

Plot buying in Karnataka is the worst high-value purchase experience urban Indians routinely undertake. Same pattern across farmland and urban sites, different specific failure modes:

- **Farmland:** PTCL claims, unconverted land, contested ownership, 79A/B restrictions, tank-bed/forest overlap, inaccurate boundaries
- **Urban sites:** disputed A-Khata/B-Khata status, unapproved layouts, BBMP vs BDA jurisdiction issues, CDP zoning violations, contested titles from partition history

In both: discovery is broker-gated, documentation is unreliable, legal blockers surface *after* money is committed. The founder spent 4 months and two failed farmland deals before starting this venture.

### 2.2 Price Inaccessibility

Stuck-inventory pattern exists in both verticals:

- **Farmland:** most parcels 5–20 acres (₹1–4 crore); largest buyer segment sits at ₹25–75L. A ₹1.5Cr 7-acre plot that four ₹40L buyers would split stays unsold for 18 months.
- **Urban sites:** ₹1.2–1.8Cr A-Khata 60×40 plots in strong Bangalore corridors; largest buyer segment sits at ₹60–80L. Two buyers could legally split a 60×40 into two 30×40s with individual khatas. Instead the plot gets bought by a developer who flips each half at a 30–40% markup.

### 2.3 Who Has These Problems

Urban professionals 30–50, from Bangalore (and increasingly Chennai, Hyderabad), buying for weekend retreat, long-term investment, retirement, home construction, or farmhouse construction. Delight Eco Farms has repeatedly seen the same customer buy an urban site and a farmland parcel within a few years. Same customer, different lifecycle moment.

Budget-wise: the ₹40–80L fractional segment is structurally underserved in both categories. Managed developers, general portals, and layout developers target higher tickets or whole-plot buyers.

## 3. The Product

### 3.1 Overview

Three interlocking layers across both categories:

1. **Verified marketplace** — aggregates listings from brokers and sellers, runs legal/factual diligence before publication
2. **Fractional facilitation** — for eligible plots, enables 3+ buyers (2+ for urban 60×40 splits) to commit advances; Delight's capital acquires; TruePlots handles subdivision and individual titling
3. **Full-stack services** — registration, conversion (farmland), khata transfer and BBMP liaison (urban), construction partnerships

### 3.2 Whole-Plot Purchase Flow

Identical across categories: discovery → Verified Report → site visit → registration → optional post-purchase services.

### 3.3 Fractional Purchase Flow

Same mechanics for both categories:

1. Listing flagged as fractional-eligible
2. Buyers commit advance on specific slice
3. Once threshold met (3 buyers for farmland, 2 buyers for urban 60×40 → 30×40 splits), Delight's capital acquires the plot
4. TruePlots executes subdivision (revenue department for farmland; BBMP for urban), files easement deeds, creates individual khatas
5. Final sale deeds between Delight and each buyer at published price

End state: each buyer owns an individually-titled parcel. Zero co-ownership entanglement.

### 3.4 Verification Engine

Two workflows in parallel:

**Farmland verification:**
- Ownership via Bhoomi / RTC
- Land classification (agricultural, converted, layout approved)
- Encumbrance via CERSAI and Karnataka EC portal
- Mutation history and chain-of-title from mother deed
- Boundary verification via KGIS
- Zoning/restrictions — PTCL, 79A/B, tank-bed, forest, greenbelt, CDP
- Road access verification
- Water source verification

**Urban site verification:**
- **A-Khata verification (mandatory at launch).** B-Khata plots excluded entirely from launch inventory — they cannot receive mainstream home loans, which breaks the fractional affordability pitch at ₹40–80L tickets.
- Layout approval order (BDA, BBMP, or BMRDA)
- Approved plan sanction and Completion Certificate (where applicable)
- Encumbrance via CERSAI and state EC portal
- Mutation history and khata transfer chain
- Boundary verification against approved layout plan
- CDP zoning and setback compliance
- BBMP property tax payment status
- Access road status (BBMP/BDA/panchayat/private)
- Water, electricity, sewage connection eligibility

Output in both cases: standardized TruePlots Verified Report PDF with clear go/no-go and risk flags.

### 3.5 Services Layer

- Final legal due diligence (partner lawyers)
- Registration — stamp duty, Kaveri, document drafting
- Land conversion (farmland, when required)
- Khata transfer and BBMP liaison (urban-specific)
- Construction design and permissions
- Caretaker and ongoing maintenance (primarily farmland)

## 4. Target Market

### 4.1 Buyer Segments

Segments defined by budget and intent, not plot category — same customer across categories at different lifecycle stages.

**Segment A — Whole-plot buyers (₹50L–₹2Cr)**
- Age 35–50, senior professionals
- Core pain: legal risk + wasted time
- What TruePlots offers: verified listings + services

**Segment B — Fractional buyers**
- Farmland: ₹25–60L tickets, age 30–45
- Urban sites: ₹60–80L tickets (elevated from earlier assumption due to A-Khata-only rule), age 30–45
- Core pain: locked out of premium inventory
- What TruePlots offers: finished individually-titled slices

**Segment C — Portfolio investors (₹1Cr+)**
- HNI, multiple parcels across categories
- Core pain: deal flow of legally clean inventory

### 4.2 Geography (Phased)

**Farmland:**
- **Phase 1 (Year 1):** Karnataka — Mandya, Maddur, Ramanagara, Kanakapura, Doddaballapur, Hosur corridor

**Urban sites:**

## 5. Competitive Landscape

### 5.1 Farmland Competitors

*Per competitive analysis v2; unchanged in v3.0.*

- **Farmland Bazaar** — emerging aggregator, 90-day fast-follow risk if they add verification
- **Saptashwa Jagati Farms** — developer, not facilitator. Different business model.
- **Managed farmland developers** — Swasya Living, Sanctity Ferme, Mytan, Hasiru, Sanjeevani, Triguna
- **No Indian player** operates verified farmland marketplace with 3-buyer-advance fractional at scale

### 5.2 Urban Site Competitors

*Per urban sites top-up analysis. This field is materially more contested than farmland.*

**Primary threat: NoBroker.**
- Venture-backed (~$300M+ raised), 6,768 active Bangalore plot listings, paid legal verification already on menu, disintermediation brand already established, high product velocity.
- **Can replicate TruePlots' verification + fractional offering within 1–2 quarters** if they see traction.
- Compresses TruePlots' urban defensibility window to **9–12 months**, not 18.

**Secondary threats:**
- 99acres, MagicBricks, Housing.com — horizontal portals with 10,000+ listings each, zero verification layer, unlikely to add fractional in year 1 (too complex for their model) but could add verification in 6 months
- Homes247, local Bangalore plot platforms — varied quality, limited velocity

**Supply-side partners (not competitors):**
- **BMRDA layout developers (NBR Group and similar)** — have stuck 60×40 inventory that matches the fractional split use case. Urban analog of the farmland stuck-inventory thesis. Partner relationship rather than competitive.

**Confirmed whitespace:**
- No Indian player operates **matchmaking + verification + joint-sale + subdivision to individual titles** as a single integrated product for urban sites. Adjacent players solve adjacent problems (fractional CRE, khata bifurcation services, horizontal portals) but none combine all four.

### 5.3 Defensibility Windows

Different per vertical:

- **Farmland:** 12–18 months uncontested. Primary threats (Hasiru, Farmland Bazaar) are slower than NoBroker.
- **Urban sites:** 9–12 months uncontested. NoBroker velocity makes this tighter.

Moat-flip thresholds:

- **Farmland:** 30+ clean fractional deals by month 12
- **Urban sites:** 20+ urban fractional deals by month 9

The moat cannot be the marketplace UI (copyable in a sprint) — it must be the operational stack: BBMP khata bifurcation playbook, licensed surveyor network, CIS-proof legal templates, deal execution SOPs.

### 5.4 Key Regulatory Context

- **Karnataka Section 79A/B (farmland)** — currently repealed, politically contested. Reversal = ~70% farmland TAM collapse. Monitored monthly.
- **PTCL Act (farmland)** — diligence checkpoint
- **BBMP A-Khata/B-Khata rules (urban sites)** — constantly evolving; B-Khata plots carry construction and financing restrictions. A-Khata-only enforced at urban launch.
- **BDA/BMRDA layout regulations** — govern which layouts are legal and sellable
- **SEBI CIS rules** — 3-buyer-advance structure stays outside CIS. Delight capital acquires; customer funds never pooled.
- **RERA (urban sites)** — applies to plotted developments >500sqm or >8 units. **Facilitator-vs-promoter classification is unresolved and critical to unit economics.** Requires specialist Karnataka RERA counsel memo before month-4 urban launch.
- **RERA (farmland)** — does not apply to agricultural land. No action required.

*"Verified plots across South India — farmland-first, urban sites alongside, all individually titled, all legally clear."*

Farmland-first brand. Urban sites appear as visible secondary category but never in hero message. Prevents brand dilution while capturing urban SEO and addressable market.

## 7. Business Model

### 7.1 Revenue Streams

Ranked by expected year-1 contribution:

1. **Transaction commission** — 1–2% of sale value, seller-side primarily
2. **Fractional coordination fee** — ₹35–60K per buyer farmland (3-buyer), ₹50–75K per buyer urban (2-buyer, higher due to BBMP subdivision complexity)
3. **Listing/verification fees** — ₹3–10K per listing charged to broker/seller
4. **Services revenue** — registration, legal escalation, conversion (farmland), khata transfer (urban), construction referral margins
5. **Premium verification tier** — ₹15–25K standalone deep-report SKU
6. **Broker subscriptions** (month 9+)

### 7.2 Unit Economics

**Whole-plot farmland deal net:** ₹1.1–2L
**Fractional farmland deal net (3-buyer, affinity channel):** ₹2.5–4L
**Whole-plot urban deal net:** ₹1–1.8L estimated
**Fractional urban deal net (2-buyer 60×40 → 30×40 split):** ₹2–3L estimated

Urban ticket sizes are higher than v2 planning (A-Khata rule pushes into ₹60–80L range) but fractional count is lower per deal (2 buyers vs 3). Net-per-deal economics end up similar to farmland whole-plot tier.

### 7.3 Capital Model

- Plot acquisition capital: Delight Eco Farms across both categories
- Working capital required in rotation: ₹6–12Cr to run 3–5 farmland + 2–3 urban deals in parallel
- TruePlots company capital: platform, team, marketing — separate

## 9. Go-to-Market Plan

### 9.1 The 12-Month Goal

- 30+ fractional farmland deals (month 12)
- 20+ whole-plot farmland deals (month 12)
- 20+ urban site deals with 10+ fractional (month 9 — compressed due to NoBroker threat)
- Operational templates locked across both categories
- Top 3 Google rankings on 5+ farmland queries, 3+ urban site queries

### 9.2 Strategic Principle

Farmland full-throttle from day 1. Urban sites layer in at month 4 conditional on three gates passing. Paid acquisition deferred until affinity CAC validated. Urban moat established by month 9 or forfeit category to NoBroker.

### 9.3 Phase 1: Farmland Validation (Months 0–4)

*Detailed execution in GTM plan v3.*

**Target:** 10 deals closed (5 whole-plot, 3–5 fractional). Affinity CAC validated. Three-gate decision at end of month 4.

### 9.4 The Three-Gate Month-4 Decision

Launch urban sites at month 4 *only if all three gates pass*:

**Gate 1 — Farmland pilot validation:**
- First 5 farmland fractional deals closed cleanly by month 3
- ≥6 of first 10 deals used affinity-channel acquisition
- Fractional buyer CAC <₹25K

**Gate 2 — RERA specialist counsel memo:**
- Karnataka RERA specialist engaged by month 2
- Written memo confirms TruePlots classified as facilitator (not promoter) under current statute
- Memo covers both farmland and urban site transactions
- If classification is ambiguous, legal restructuring completed before launch

**Gate 3 — Urban supply density validation:**
- Mystery-shop across Devanahalli, Sarjapur, Whitefield periphery, Kanakapura Road, Bannerghatta
- Confirms >100 A-Khata plots available per month in ₹60–80L range across target corridors
- Validates BMRDA layout developer stuck-inventory thesis (first 3+ developers open to supply partnership)

**If all three gates pass:** urban sites launch month 4–6.
**If any gate fails:** urban launch delays to month 6–9. Farmland focus continues. Gate re-run at month 6.

### 9.5 Phase 2: Dual Track (Months 5–8)

**Farmland scaling:** referral engine, SEO content (10+/month), founder content, Delight customer referrals, WhatsApp community seeding, paid acquisition experiments.

**Urban launch:** first 20 A-Khata sites live, first 5 urban SEO cluster pieces, first 2–3 whole-plot urban deals in pipeline, urban fractional pilots (2-buyer 60×40 splits) from month 7.

### 9.6 Phase 3: Moat-Flip Execution (Months 9–12)

- 30+ farmland fractional deals cumulative
- 20+ urban site deals with 10+ fractional cumulative by month 9
- Tamil Nadu farmland launch
- Urban expansion to 3rd Bangalore corridor
- Managed farmland developer partnerships
- BMRDA layout developer supply partnerships formalized
- PR push: category-creator positioning in Economic Times, YourStory, Inc42

## 11. Risks and Open Questions

### 11.1 Top Risks

1. **NoBroker fast-follow on urban sites.** 1–2 quarter replication risk. Mitigation: 20+ urban fractional deals by month 9; moat lives in operational stack, not UI.
2. **RERA facilitator-vs-promoter classification unresolved for urban.** Unit-economics-critical. Mitigation: specialist counsel memo before month-4 launch (Gate 2).
3. **Section 79A/B restoration** in Karnataka. ~70% farmland TAM collapse scenario. Monitor monthly.
4. **Vertical sprawl.** Farmland + urban simultaneously risks fragmenting attention. Mitigation: three-gate sequencing; urban lags 4 months; farmland full-throttle until stable.
5. **Affinity channel burnout.** 5000+ network not infinitely convertible. Discipline on personal, relevant outreach only.
6. **First fractional deal failure.** One public subdivision problem damages brand 12 months. Mitigation: over-invest in first 3 deals, closest-circle buyers only, senior Delight involvement.
7. **B-Khata exclusion narrows urban TAM ~30–50%.** Mitigation: launch A-Khata-only; evaluate B-Khata inclusion post month 12 once A-Khata traction proven.
8. **Founder bandwidth.** Dual-category execution makes Land Ops + Urban Ops hires non-negotiable.

### 11.2 Open Questions

1. Exit/resale mechanism for fractional buyers (both categories) — when buyer #2 wants to sell 1.5 years later, how does the platform handle it?
2. Saptashwa deep-dive — mystery-shop legal structure and margin stack
3. Karnataka Legislative Council movement on 79A/B
4. Buyer willingness-to-pay for urban fractional coordination fee (₹50–75K assumption untested)
5. Fundraising timing — ₹68–103L year-1 budget is bootstrappable but tight; ₹3–5cr seed around month 5–6 creates comfort and funds Urban Ops hire
6. BMRDA layout developer partnership economics — what revenue split motivates them to route stuck inventory through TruePlots?
7. RERA memo output — if facilitator classification is refused, what does the legal restructuring look like?
