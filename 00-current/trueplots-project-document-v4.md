## 1. Executive Summary

TruePlots is a verified plots marketplace for South India with built-in fractional access for buyers who cannot afford premium plots alone. Every listing is legally verified before buyers see it. Plots too large for a single buyer are sold as individual slices once buyer thresholds are met, with TruePlots facilitating full legal separation — subdivision, easement deeds, individual titles.

Supply comes primarily through a curated agent network: Delight Realty (Eshwar) brings 100+ trusted agents to the platform at launch. Every listing is jointly verified by the listing agent and TruePlots' legal team against the 120-point checklist before going live. Premium inventory moves to tripartite exclusive selling agreements (owner + TruePlots + agent) that unlock higher combined margins and platform marketing commitment.

Two plot categories are in scope, sequenced deliberately:

- **Farmland (hero product)** — rural parcels across Karnataka, verified against revenue-department records. Launches month 0. 100-200 parcels from day one via Delight's agent network.
- **Urban site plots (secondary, conditional)** — A-Khata residential plots in Bangalore peripheral corridors, verified against BBMP/BDA records. Launches month 4 *only if* three gates pass.

Farmland leads because the moat is clearest (Delight's rural legal depth, underserved ₹25-60L fractional segment, CIS-proof 3-buyer model, thin competitive field, and now Delight Realty's agent network). Urban sites extend addressable market — but compete against a venture-backed fast-mover (NoBroker) with a 9-12 month window to establish operational lead.

**Year-1 goal:** 30+ fractional farmland deals + 20+ whole-plot farmland deals + 20+ urban site deals (of which 10+ fractional) by month 9. The month-9 urban milestone is compressed from earlier planning due to NoBroker fast-follow risk.

## 2. The Problem

### 2.1 Legal and Informational Fraud

Plot buying in Karnataka is the worst high-value purchase experience urban Indians routinely undertake. Same pattern across farmland and urban sites, different specific failure modes:

- **Farmland:** PTCL claims, unconverted land, contested ownership, 79A/B restrictions, tank-bed/forest overlap, inaccurate boundaries
- **Urban sites:** disputed A-Khata/B-Khata status, unapproved layouts, BBMP vs BDA jurisdiction issues, CDP zoning violations, contested titles from partition history

In both: discovery is broker-gated, documentation is unreliable, legal blockers surface *after* money is committed. The founder spent 4 months and two failed farmland deals before starting this venture.

### 2.2 Price Inaccessibility

Stuck-inventory pattern exists in both verticals:

- **Farmland:** most parcels 5-20 acres (₹1-4 crore); largest buyer segment sits at ₹25-75L. A ₹1.5Cr 7-acre plot that four ₹40L buyers would split stays unsold for 18 months.
- **Urban sites:** ₹1.2-1.8Cr A-Khata 60×40 plots in strong Bangalore corridors; largest buyer segment sits at ₹60-80L. Two buyers could legally split a 60×40 into two 30×40s with individual khatas. Instead the plot gets bought by a developer who flips each half at a 30-40% markup.

### 2.3 Who Has These Problems

Urban professionals 30-50, from Bangalore (and increasingly Chennai, Hyderabad), buying for weekend retreat, long-term investment, retirement, home construction, or farmhouse construction. Delight Eco Farms has repeatedly seen the same customer buy an urban site and a farmland parcel within a few years. Same customer, different lifecycle moment.

Budget-wise: the ₹40-80L fractional segment is structurally underserved in both categories. Managed developers, general portals, and layout developers target higher tickets or whole-plot buyers.

### 2.4 The Organization Gap (New Framing)

The Karnataka plots market has two structural failures, and framing them separately clarifies the product:

- **Farmland: a demand-supply gap.** Qualified buyers cannot find trusted sellers. Trusted sellers cannot reach qualified buyers. Agents exist but operate in fragmented local networks with no discovery layer for buyers outside their circle.
- **Urban: an organization gap.** Inventory is plentiful (99acres alone has 10,000+ Bangalore listings) but chaotic. Buyers cannot distinguish clean plots from problematic ones. Agents exist but are commodity-treated.

TruePlots solves both with a verified-supply + verified-agent + verified-buyer architecture. We don't fight the existing agent system (NoBroker's bet); we organize it.

## 3. The Product

### 3.1 Overview

Three interlocking layers across both categories:

1. **Verified marketplace** — aggregates listings from agents, runs legal/factual diligence before publication, presents verified inventory to buyers
2. **Fractional facilitation** — for eligible plots, enables 3+ buyers (2+ for urban 60×40 splits) to commit advances; Delight's capital acquires; TruePlots handles subdivision and individual titling
3. **Full-stack services** — registration, conversion (farmland), khata transfer and BBMP liaison (urban), construction partnerships

### 3.2 Agent-Inclusive Marketplace Model (New in v4)

Platform architecture now includes three parties in every transaction: buyer, agent, TruePlots (with Delight Eco Farms as operational parent and Delight Realty as agent-network partner).

**How listings get created:**
- Agents bring curated inventory. Each agent has a relationship with plot owners and a view of what's available in their territory.
- Every plot is jointly verified by the listing agent and TruePlots' verification team against the 120-point farmland checklist (or ~80-point urban checklist) before going live.
- Agents cannot self-publish. TruePlots is the final verification gate — verification failures block listings.
- Listings attribute both parties: "Listed by [Agent Name] · Verified by TruePlots" appears on listing cards and LDPs.

**Two commercial tiers:**
- **Standard listings** — non-exclusive, agent retains rights to sell through other channels. Lower combined commission. Default.
- **Premium / exclusive listings** — tripartite agreement (owner + TruePlots + agent) grants exclusive selling rights to the platform-agent partnership. Higher combined commission. Marketing commitment from TruePlots (priority placement, dedicated content, paid promotion). Reserved for top inventory.

**Agent verification is a first-class product feature:**
- RERA registration check
- Track record (years active, transactions closed, geography)
- References (three landowners + three past buyers)
- Ongoing performance scorecard — dispute rate, listing-to-close ratio, buyer satisfaction, time-to-close
- Public agent profiles appear on the platform from month 6 onward
- Agents with repeated quality failures get removed

**Why this matters:**
- Supply scales through the agent network, not founder field work
- Trust layer applies at every step: verified plots + verified agents + platform-owned verification process
- Agents get platform-provided buyers, verification infrastructure, payment rails, and documented process in exchange for commission share
- Buyers get transparent attribution and a scorecard to evaluate agent quality

### 3.3 Whole-Plot Purchase Flow

Identical across categories: discovery → Verified Report → site visit → registration → optional post-purchase services. Agent attribution and buyer communication happen alongside the platform; agent remains point-of-contact for property-specific context.

### 3.4 Fractional Purchase Flow

Same mechanics for both categories:

1. Listing flagged as fractional-eligible by agent + TruePlots
2. Buyers commit advance on specific slice
3. Once threshold met (3 buyers for farmland, 2 buyers for urban 60×40 → 30×40 splits), Delight's capital acquires the plot
4. TruePlots executes subdivision (revenue department for farmland; BBMP for urban), files easement deeds, creates individual khatas
5. Final sale deeds between Delight and each buyer at published price
6. Commission paid to listing agent at close

End state: each buyer owns an individually-titled parcel. Zero co-ownership entanglement.

### 3.5 Verification Engine

Two workflows in parallel:

**Farmland verification:**
- Ownership via Bhoomi / RTC
- Land classification (agricultural, converted, layout approved)
- Encumbrance via CERSAI and Karnataka EC portal
- Mutation history and chain-of-title from mother deed
- Boundary verification via KGIS
- Zoning/restrictions — PTCL, 79A/B, tank-bed, forest, greenbelt, CDP
- Road access verification
- Water source verification

**Urban site verification:**
- **A-Khata verification (mandatory at launch).** B-Khata plots excluded entirely from launch inventory — they cannot receive mainstream home loans, which breaks the fractional affordability pitch at ₹40-80L tickets.
- Layout approval order (BDA, BBMP, or BMRDA)
- Approved plan sanction and Completion Certificate (where applicable)
- Encumbrance via CERSAI and state EC portal
- Mutation history and khata transfer chain
- Boundary verification against approved layout plan
- CDP zoning and setback compliance
- BBMP property tax payment status
- Access road status (BBMP/BDA/panchayat/private)
- Water, electricity, sewage connection eligibility

Output in both cases: standardized TruePlots Verified Report PDF with clear go/no-go and risk flags. Report is branded TruePlots; agent is credited on the cover page.

### 3.6 Services Layer

- Final legal due diligence (partner lawyers)
- Registration — stamp duty, Kaveri, document drafting
- Land conversion (farmland, when required)
- Khata transfer and BBMP liaison (urban-specific)
- Construction design and permissions
- Caretaker and ongoing maintenance (primarily farmland)

## 5. Competitive Landscape

### 5.1 Farmland Competitors

*Per competitive analysis v2; unchanged in v4.*

- **Farmland Bazaar** — emerging aggregator, 90-day fast-follow risk if they add verification
- **Saptashwa Jagati Farms** — developer, not facilitator. Different business model.
- **Managed farmland developers** — Swasya Living, Sanctity Ferme, Mytan, Hasiru, Sanjeevani, Triguna
- **No Indian player** operates verified farmland marketplace with 3-buyer-advance fractional at scale

### 5.2 Urban Site Competitors

*Per urban sites top-up analysis. This field is materially more contested than farmland.*

**Primary threat: NoBroker.**
- Venture-backed (~$300M+ raised), 6,768 active Bangalore plot listings, paid legal verification already on menu, disintermediation brand already established, high product velocity.
- **Can replicate TruePlots' verification + fractional offering within 1-2 quarters** if they see traction.
- Compresses TruePlots' urban defensibility window to **9-12 months**, not 18.
- NoBroker's strategic bet is to remove agents. TruePlots' bet is to organize them. These are different product positions — a buyer who values agent relationships and on-ground trust will not be served by NoBroker, even at scale.

**Secondary threats:**
- 99acres, MagicBricks, Housing.com — horizontal portals with 10,000+ listings each, zero verification layer, unlikely to add fractional in year 1 (too complex for their model) but could add verification in 6 months
- Homes247, local Bangalore plot platforms — varied quality, limited velocity

**Supply-side partners (not competitors):**
- **BMRDA layout developers (NBR Group and similar)** — have stuck 60×40 inventory that matches the fractional split use case. Urban analog of the farmland stuck-inventory thesis. Partner relationship rather than competitive.

**Confirmed whitespace:**
- No Indian player operates **verified listings + verified agents + matchmaking + joint-sale + subdivision to individual titles** as a single integrated product. Adjacent players solve adjacent problems (fractional CRE, khata bifurcation services, horizontal portals, agent-removing marketplaces) but none combine all four.

### 5.3 Competitive Positioning

The Karnataka plots market has two structural failures. Farmland: a demand-supply gap — qualified buyers cannot find trusted sellers, trusted sellers cannot reach qualified buyers. Urban: an organization gap — inventory is plentiful but chaotic, buyers cannot distinguish clean plots from problematic ones. TruePlots solves both with a verified-supply + verified-agent + verified-buyer architecture. 99acres and MagicBricks optimize for volume and do neither job well. NoBroker removed agents entirely, which left small-ticket rentals but failed in plots where local relationships and on-ground verification matter. TruePlots sits between the two: we include agents but organize them, and we own verification end-to-end.

Positioning line: **"We don't fight the existing system, we organize it. Agents get products, we get customers."**

### 5.4 Defensibility Windows

Different per vertical:

- **Farmland:** 12-18 months uncontested. Primary threats (Hasiru, Farmland Bazaar) are slower than NoBroker.
- **Urban sites:** 9-12 months uncontested. NoBroker velocity makes this tighter.

Moat-flip thresholds:

- **Farmland:** 30+ clean fractional deals by month 12
- **Urban sites:** 20+ urban fractional deals by month 9

The moat cannot be the marketplace UI (copyable in a sprint) — it must be the operational stack: verified-agent network, BBMP khata bifurcation playbook, licensed surveyor network, CIS-proof legal templates, deal execution SOPs.

### 5.5 Key Regulatory Context

- **Karnataka Section 79A/B (farmland)** — currently repealed, politically contested. Reversal = ~70% farmland TAM collapse. Monitored monthly.
- **PTCL Act (farmland)** — diligence checkpoint
- **BBMP A-Khata/B-Khata rules (urban sites)** — constantly evolving; B-Khata plots carry construction and financing restrictions. A-Khata-only enforced at urban launch.
- **BDA/BMRDA layout regulations** — govern which layouts are legal and sellable
- **SEBI CIS rules** — 3-buyer-advance structure stays outside CIS. Delight capital acquires; customer funds never pooled.
- **RERA (urban sites)** — applies to plotted developments >500sqm or >8 units. **Facilitator-vs-promoter classification is unresolved and critical to unit economics.** Requires specialist Karnataka RERA counsel memo before month-4 urban launch.
- **RERA (farmland)** — does not apply to agricultural land. No action required.
- **RERA (agents)** — listing agents must be RERA-registered where statute applies. Agent verification gate enforces this.

## 6. Positioning

### 6.1 Statement

*"Verified plots across South India — farmland-first, urban sites alongside, all listed by verified agents, all individually titled, all legally clear."*

### 6.2 Strategic Choice

Farmland-first brand. Urban sites appear as visible secondary category but never in hero message. Prevents brand dilution while capturing urban SEO and addressable market.

### 6.3 Messaging Architecture

Three pillars ranked:

1. **Access** — "Premium plots from ₹40 lakh (farmland) / ₹60 lakh (urban). No pooling. No strangers. Your plot, titled in your name."
2. **Verification** — "Every plot legally checked before you see it. Every agent verified before they list. Farmland or urban site."
3. **End-to-end** — "From discovery to individual khata, we handle everything."

### 6.4 Vertical-Specific Messaging

**Farmland:** *"Verified farmland across Karnataka, listed by verified agents. When 3 buyers commit, we handle the full legal separation — you get your own individually titled plot within 30 days."*

**Urban sites:** *"Verified A-Khata plots across Bangalore, listed by verified agents. Two buyers can split a 60×40 into clean 30×40s, each with its own khata. We handle the subdivision legally."*

### 6.5 Counter-Positioning

**vs developers and layout sellers:** *"Developers build gated communities. Layout sellers sell layouts. We're a marketplace — choose any verified plot across Karnataka, pay market rate, own it in your name."*

**vs NoBroker (urban specific):** *"NoBroker removed agents. TruePlots verifies them. Plots are not rentals — local knowledge, on-ground relationships, and trust matter. You see who is selling and how they've performed. Then we verify the plot."*

**vs 99acres/MagicBricks:** *"Horizontal portals list everything. We verify everything. Same inventory density, zero buyer risk."*

### 6.6 Moat

Our moat is the combination of four operational assets, not any one feature:

1. **Delight's 100-agent network** — six years of Karnataka farmland operations, trust relationships that competitors cannot buy with capital. Delight Realty's partnership converts this into TruePlots' launch supply in month 1.
2. **120-point verification process** — codified, taught, enforced. Every listing. Every agent.
3. **Individual-title fractional mechanics** — CIS-proof 3-buyer advance structure; Delight's balance sheet; subdivision playbook; individual khatas. No Indian competitor replicates this for plots.
4. **Full-stack services layer** — registration, conversion, khata transfer, construction. Revenue expansion beyond commission and stickiness beyond one transaction.

Competitors (NoBroker, Farmland Bazaar, MagicBricks) would need 18+ months to replicate any single layer. Replicating all four requires a strategic pivot they are not positioned to make.

## 7. Business Model

### 7.1 Revenue Streams

Ranked by expected year-1 contribution:

1. **Transaction commission (standard listings)** — combined buyer-side + seller-side, exact split TBD pending Eshwar meeting #2. Agent gets 1% on sales originated through their listings. TruePlots platform fee TBD (likely 1-2%).
2. **Transaction commission (exclusive tripartite listings)** — higher combined rate, reflects marketing commitment and selling-rights exclusivity. Split TBD pending Eshwar meeting #2.
3. **Fractional coordination fee** — ₹35-60K per buyer farmland (3-buyer), ₹50-75K per buyer urban (2-buyer, higher due to BBMP subdivision complexity)
4. **Listing/verification fees** — may be built into commission; standalone SKU for unlisted verification (buyer-pays deep-report)
5. **Services revenue** — registration, legal escalation, conversion (farmland), khata transfer (urban), construction referral margins
6. **Premium verification tier** — ₹15-25K standalone deep-report SKU
7. **Loan origination referral fees** — NBFC partners, paid at disbursal
8. **Broker / agent subscriptions** (month 9+) — toolset access, lead routing, featured placement

### 7.2 TBD — Pending Eshwar Meeting #2

The following commercial terms are open and will be finalized in the second Eshwar meeting:

- Exact commission split between agent, TruePlots, and Delight (on standard vs exclusive listings)
- Buyer-side vs seller-side payment mechanics (who pays commission, disclosed how)
- Cross-agent referral economics (does agent A get paid when buyer introduced by agent A closes on agent B's listing?)
- Liability structure — who bears loss if verified plot later turns out to have title defect? (Baseline: TruePlots offers platform-level title indemnity; agent liable for material misrepresentation; Delight absorbs subdivision execution risk)
- Bad-agent removal mechanics — scorecard thresholds, process, appeal path
- Exclusive listing agreement template — term, carve-outs, marketing SLA, termination triggers

Until these land, numbers in this document are directional.

### 7.3 Unit Economics

**Whole-plot farmland deal net (after agent commission):** ₹0.7-1.5L (down from v3 ₹1.1-2L as agent commission moves in)
**Fractional farmland deal net (3-buyer, affinity channel):** ₹1.8-3.2L
**Whole-plot urban deal net:** ₹0.6-1.4L estimated
**Fractional urban deal net (2-buyer 60×40 → 30×40 split):** ₹1.5-2.5L estimated

Lower per-deal net than v3 is offset by two effects: (a) deal volume multiplier from 100-agent network vs founder-sourced supply, and (b) exclusive tripartite listings recapture margin on top inventory.

### 7.4 Capital Model

- Plot acquisition capital: Delight Eco Farms across both categories
- Working capital required in rotation: ₹6-12Cr to run 3-5 farmland + 2-3 urban deals in parallel
- TruePlots company capital: platform, team, marketing — separate
- Agent network cost to TruePlots: approximately zero (agents bring supply, get commission on close, no upfront payment)
