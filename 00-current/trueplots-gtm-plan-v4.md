## 1. The Starting Position

TruePlots launches past the cold-start problem on two fronts. Four months of founder field work has produced a playbook and baseline 50+ verified farmland parcels across Mandya, Maddur, Ramanagara. Delight Eco Farms adds farmland legal ops, subdivision expertise, sub-registrar relationships, plus BBMP/BDA-side experience from their layout development work that enables urban sites as a second vertical.

**New in v4:** Delight Realty (Eshwar) partnership adds a 100+ trusted real estate agent network as the primary supply engine. Launch-day target moves from 50+ founder-sourced listings to 100-200 agent-sourced + jointly-verified listings. Agent onboarding starts week 1 and runs as a parallel track alongside the buyer-facing build.

The 5000+ warm founder network remains a high-affinity buyer pool for the critical commercial risk (stranger-pool CAC in fractional deals). Agent-sourced leads come online as a second buyer channel from month 3.

**Sequencing:** Farmland full-throttle from day 1. Agent onboarding runs in parallel (month 1 activation, month 2 first cohort live). Urban sites gated through three checks at month 4. If all three pass, urban launches months 4-6. If any fails, urban delays to month 6-9 and re-gates.

## 3. Strategic Framework

### 3.1 Positioning

Farmland-first hero, urban sites as secondary category.

Hero: *"Own premium farmland from ₹40 lakh."*
Sub-hero: *"Verified plots across South India. Farmland and urban sites. Listed by verified agents. Individually titled. Legally clear."*

Category-level framing (for press, analyst conversations, investor pitches): *"We don't fight the existing system, we organize it. Agents get products, we get customers."*

### 3.2 Sequencing Principle

**Farmland full-throttle from day 1. Agent onboarding starts week 1 as parallel workstream. Urban sites gated month 4.** Reasons:

- Farmland supply is ready and agent-sourcing scales it immediately; urban supply still needs A-Khata validation
- Farmland moat is clearer; urban sites face NoBroker fast-follow
- Farmland operational model needs validation before dual-track attention
- RERA classification must be resolved before urban transactions
- Agent workflow must be proven on farmland before extending to urban BBMP khata complexity

### 3.3 Competitive Compression

Defensibility windows differ:

- **Farmland:** 12-18 months uncontested. Primary threats (Hasiru, Farmland Bazaar) are slower. Delight's 100-agent network is structurally unreplicable — six years of local trust cannot be bought.
- **Urban sites:** 9-12 months uncontested. NoBroker has $300M+ raised, 6,768 Bangalore plot listings, paid verification upsell live, and product velocity that could replicate TruePlots' offering in 1-2 quarters. Note: NoBroker's thesis *removes* agents; TruePlots' thesis *verifies* agents. Different buyer fits.

**Urban sites moat cannot be the marketplace UI** — that's copyable in a sprint. The moat must be the operational stack: verified-agent network + BBMP khata bifurcation playbook + licensed surveyor network + CIS-proof legal templates + deal execution SOPs. Execute 20+ urban fractional deals by month 9 or forfeit the category.

## 5. Phase 1: Farmland Validation + Agent Scaling (Months 1-4)

**Goal:** 10 deals closed (5 whole-plot, 3-5 fractional). Affinity CAC validated. 20+ active agents with 50+ listings by end of month 2. Three-gate decision at end of month 4.

### Month 1 — Activation

- Platform live with 50-100+ farmland listings
- 10 agents onboarded (first Delight Realty cohort)
- Personal broadcast to top 500 network contacts
- First 5 site visits, founder-led (with listing agent in the loop)
- First fractional plot activated (7-acre Mandya pitched to 4-5 warm buyers)
- 2 whole-plot deals advancing
- RERA counsel engaged; memo drafting begins
- **Agent KPI:** 10 onboarded, 50+ listings live, ~5 listings per agent average

### Month 2 — First Closes + Agent Cohort 2

- 2 whole-plot farmland deals close
- First fractional deal activates (3 buyers committed, Delight acquires)
- Second broadcast wave with social proof
- Founder LinkedIn Diary begins
- **Agent Success Lead hire** (dedicated role: onboarding, scorecards, quality management, weekly check-ins)
- Next 10 agents onboarded (running total: 20)
- First listings flagged as fractional-eligible by agents
- RERA memo draft received and reviewed
- **Agent KPI:** 20 active agents, 100+ listings, first 2 listings-to-close attributed to agents

### Month 3 — Referral Activation + Agent Scorecard Foundation

- First buyer case studies live
- Referral program launched (₹25K per closed referral; agent referral bonus TBD)
- Delight Eco Farms farmland customer base receives first-look offering
- Third broadcast wave
- Agent public scorecard data model defined (dispute rate, listing-to-close ratio, time-to-close, buyer satisfaction)
- First performance data collected for onboarded agents
- Urban supply mystery-shop begins (Gate 3 input) — in parallel, 5+ Delight Realty agents with Bangalore urban coverage flagged for pre-onboarding
- Agent landing page + agent application flow goes live
- **Agent KPI:** 30 active agents, 150+ listings, first agents showing early performance signal

### Month 4 — Three-Gate Decision

**Target:** 10 deals closed cumulative. All three gates evaluated. 40+ active agents, 200+ listings.

**Gate 1 — Farmland pilot validation:**
- ≥6 of 10 deals had majority-affinity acquisition (agent-sourced buyers count as affinity for this purpose)
- First 5 farmland fractional deals closed cleanly (no subdivision defects, no title issues surfaced)
- Fractional buyer CAC <₹25K
- Agent onboarding hitting plan (≥20 agents by month 2 achieved)
- First 1-2 tripartite exclusive agreements signed

**Gate 2 — RERA classification confirmed:**
- Written memo from Karnataka RERA specialist confirming TruePlots as facilitator, not promoter, under current statute
- Memo covers both farmland and urban site transactions
- Memo addresses platform liability in agent-listed transactions
- Unit economics hold under the confirmed classification

**Gate 3 — Urban supply density validated:**
- Mystery-shop confirms >100 A-Khata plots per month in ₹60-80L range across Devanahalli, Sarjapur, Whitefield periphery, Kanakapura Road, Bannerghatta
- 3+ BMRDA layout developers open to supply partnership for stuck 60×40 inventory
- 15+ Delight Realty agents covering Bangalore urban corridors pre-identified and briefed on urban launch

**Decision tree:**

- **All three pass:** urban sites launch Phase 2, month 4-6. Hire Urban Site Ops Lead immediately.
- **Any gate fails:** urban launch delays to month 6-9. Farmland focus continues. Re-gate at month 6.
- **Farmland Gate 1 fails (red on affinity):** fractional product redesign required before any urban expansion considered.

2. **RERA facilitator-vs-promoter classification unresolved** — blocks clean urban launch. Memo must also cover platform liability in agent-listed transactions.

6. **Vertical sprawl** — three-gate sequencing mitigates
7. **Affinity channel burnout** — disciplined outreach required; agent channel partially offsets
8. **First fractional deal failure** — over-invest in first 3 deals
9. **B-Khata exclusion narrows urban TAM** — mitigation: A-Khata-only at launch
10. **Founder bandwidth** — Land Ops + Agent Success + Urban Ops Lead by month 4-5 non-negotiable

### 10.2 Decision Gates

- **Month 1:** Eshwar meeting #2 — commission splits, liability, exclusivity mechanics locked.
- **Month 4:** three-gate evaluation (farmland pilots + RERA memo + urban supply). If all green, urban launches. If any fails, urban delays to month 6-9.

- **Month 6 (if urban delayed):** three-gate re-evaluation for urban launch.
