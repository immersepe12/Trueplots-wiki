## What we're building

A marketplace for plots in South India where every listing is legally verified before the buyer sees it, every listing agent is vetted before they can post, and where plots too large for a single buyer can be jointly purchased with others — with full legal subdivision, easement deeds, and individual titles handled end-to-end.

Farmland is the hero. Delight Eco Farms' six-year operational partnership provides the legal and subdivision muscle. Delight Realty's 100+ agent network provides the supply. TruePlots owns verification, trust, and the buyer-facing product. The same infrastructure extends naturally to urban site plots — A-Khata 30×40s, 40×60s, and 60×40s across Bangalore peripheral corridors — where the same two problems (legal fraud, price inaccessibility) exist in a different wrapper.

A buyer picks a plot. Three buyers commit advances (two for urban 60×40 → 30×40 splits). Our capital acquires the plot. We handle the legal separation. Each buyer ends up with their own individually-titled slice.

No pooling. No strangers. No coordination risk.

## The problem

Plot buying in Karnataka — farmland or urban site — is the worst high-value purchase experience urban Indians routinely undertake. Two independent problems exist, and no existing player solves both:

**1. Legal and informational fraud.** Discovery is broker-gated. Documentation is unreliable. Farmland buyers get blindsided by PTCL claims, unconverted land, contested ownership, 79A/B restrictions. Urban site buyers get blindsided by disputed A-Khata/B-Khata status, contested layouts, BBMP vs BDA jurisdiction issues, unapproved subdivisions. All of it surfaces *after* a buyer has committed money.

**2. Price inaccessibility.** A ₹1.5Cr 7-acre farmland plot that four ₹40L buyers would split stays unsold. A ₹1.5Cr A-Khata 60×40 urban site that two ₹75L buyers could divide into two 30×40s stays unsold. Same stuck-inventory pattern, different scale. Nobody coordinates the split.

Underneath both: an **organization gap**. Farmland has a demand-supply gap — qualified buyers can't find trusted sellers. Urban has an organization gap — plenty of inventory, nothing is filterable or trustable. Agents exist but are commodity-treated or removed entirely. The market needs someone to organize them, not replace them.

The founder spent four months and two failed farmland deals before deciding to solve this as a business.

## The model

TruePlots operates as three interlocking layers across both plot categories:

1. **Verified marketplace with verified agents.** Every plot checked — farmland against Bhoomi, KGIS, PTCL, encumbrance, 79A/B status; urban sites against BBMP/BDA records, A-Khata status (B-Khata plots excluded at launch), CDP zoning, approved layout orders. Every agent checked — RERA registration, track record, references, ongoing public scorecard. Buyers receive a standardized TruePlots Verified Report with every plot, credited to both TruePlots and the listing agent.
2. **Fractional facilitation.** Plots too large for one buyer sold as slices once buyer thresholds are met. Delight's capital acquires the plot. TruePlots handles subdivision and individual titling.
3. **Full-stack services.** Registration, conversion (farmland), khata transfer (urban), construction partnerships.

**Positioning:** *"We don't fight the existing system, we organize it. Agents get products, we get customers."*

**Delight Eco Farms is our parent and equity partner.** Years of farmland subdivision, layout development, sub-registrar work, and BBMP-side experience mean TruePlots launches with in-house legal and operational capability that a standalone startup takes 18 months to build. Plot acquisition capital comes from Delight's balance sheet.

**Delight Realty brings a 100+ trusted agent network.** TruePlots launches with 100-200 verified farmland plots from day one via partnership with Delight Realty. Delight's 100+ trusted agent network, built over six years of Karnataka real estate operations, becomes TruePlots' curated supply engine. Every plot is jointly verified by the listing agent and TruePlots' legal team against 120 checkpoints before going live. This is supply-side infrastructure competitors cannot replicate by writing checks — six years of trust-building is the input.

**The moat is verification at every layer — not any single feature.** Our moat is not verified plots alone. It's the combination of verified plots, verified agents, individually-titled fractional mechanics, and a transparent marketplace that attributes every listing and scores every participant. 99acres has volume. NoBroker removed agents entirely — which works for rentals but fails where local relationships and on-ground trust matter most. We include agents but organize them, and we own verification end-to-end.

**The 3-buyer-advance structure is cleanly outside regulated territory.** Customer funds are never pooled to acquire assets (the failure mode SEBI cited when shutting Growpital down in 2024). Customers don't face coordination risk. We don't face inventory risk.

**Operational stack, not UI, is the moat.** Marketplace UIs can be copied in a sprint. The defensibility lives in the agent network, BBMP khata bifurcation playbook, licensed surveyor network, CIS-proof legal templates, and deal execution SOPs. This takes 9-12 months minimum to build from scratch — and the agent network alone requires six years.

## Honest risks

- **NoBroker fast-follow on urban sites.** They could replicate verification + fractional in 1-2 quarters. Mitigation: 20+ urban fractional deals by month 9, moat in operational stack + agent network not UI. Note their thesis removes agents while ours verifies them — different buyer fits.
- **Regulatory.** Section 79A/B in Karnataka (farmland) is currently repealed but politically contested. Reversal would collapse ~70% of farmland TAM; we'd accelerate Tamil Nadu and lean harder into urban sites.
- **RERA classification.** Urban plot fractional may fall under RERA if classified as "promoter" rather than "facilitator." We're resolving this via Karnataka RERA specialist counsel memo before any urban launch. Memo also covers platform liability in agent-listed transactions.
- **Partnership risk.** Delight Realty commercial terms (commission splits, liability, exclusivity) TBD pending Eshwar meeting #2. Mitigated by strong existing relationship — Kushal's brother operates Delight Eco Farms; Eshwar is sibling-network known to the founders.
- **Agent quality risk.** A title defect on an agent-listed plot damages brand even with joint verification. Mitigation: TruePlots is final verification gate, platform-level title indemnity, removal-on-pattern, public scorecard.
- **Vertical sprawl.** Running farmland and urban sites simultaneously risks fragmenting founder attention. Mitigation: strict three-gate sequencing at month 4; farmland full-throttle until stable.
- **Unit economics.** The assumption that warm-network + agent-sourced acquisition reliably delivers committed buyers per fractional deal is validated on pilots but not at scale. Month-4 gate either greenlights scale or forces redesign.
- **Category history.** Most Indian farmland investment startups have failed. Growpital was shut down by SEBI in 2024. We've engineered carefully around that specific failure mode.
