## What 99acres is — and what it isn't

99acres is a listings portal. Sellers (brokers, owners, developers) pay to list; buyers browse and contact sellers directly. The portal does not verify land records, does not check legal risks, does not structure the transaction, and does not offer services beyond advertising.

For residential flats and plotted layouts in developed zones, 99acres' model works fine — RERA regulates the developer, title is usually clean, and the buyer-side legal diligence is straightforward.

**For agricultural and farm land, the model breaks down.** There is no RERA coverage. Sellers are not regulated. Titles routinely carry PTCL risk, tenancy claims, unrecorded encumbrances, incorrect boundaries on Bhoomi, and 79A/B applicability issues — none of which 99acres filters, flags, or verifies.

## How TruePlots is structured differently

Every TruePlots listing goes through a verification pipeline before publication:

- **Ownership verification** via Bhoomi / RTC lookup
- **Land classification** (agricultural, converted, layout approved) via state records
- **Encumbrance status** via CERSAI and Karnataka EC portal
- **Mutation history** and chain-of-title reconstruction from mother deed
- **Boundary verification** via KGIS cross-checked against claimed boundaries
- **PTCL and zoning** flags (tank bed, forest land, 79A/B applicability, greenbelt, CDP zoning)
- **Road access verification** (private/revenue/panchayat road, kharab)
- **Water source verification** (borewell, open well, proximity)
- **Downloadable verification report** summarising findings and flagging risks

Listings that fail verification or carry unresolvable legal risks are rejected before they reach buyers. Listings with fixable issues are surfaced with explicit risk flags.
