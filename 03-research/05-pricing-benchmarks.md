## 1. Transaction commission

- **Indian real estate brokerage standard:** 1–2% each side (buyer + seller), often negotiated to 1% on large transactions. Joint commission is 2–3% total. Source: anecdotal industry norm; NoBroker charges 0% (zero-brokerage disruptor model).
- **AcreTrader:** ~2.5% formation fee at closing + 0.75% annual + 5% disposition. [themodestwallet.com](https://themodestwallet.com/acretrader-review/)
- **FarmTogether:** ~2% upfront + 1–2% annual + NOI share + carry.
- **Harvest Returns:** Sponsor-pays model — zero investor fees.
- **PropShare SM REIT:** Fee schedule governed by offer document; SEBI caps upfront load.

**Recommended range for TruePlots:** **1.5–2.5% of sale value, primarily seller-side**, capped at ₹3–5 lakh on deals over ₹2 cr (to stay competitive on large tickets where absolute fee matters more than %).

## 2. Listing / verification fees to seller

- No direct India benchmark. 99acres / MagicBricks charge sellers ₹3K–₹10K for featured listings.
- The verification cost to TruePlots per listing (Bhoomi lookup + KGIS + lawyer review + EC) is probably ₹2K–5K at internal cost, ₹8K–12K if outsourced.
- Sellers/brokers will resist paying unless the verification finds/fixes a real issue.

**Recommended:** **₹5–7K baseline for standard listing + verification** (covers cost + modest margin). Free for early broker partnerships to drive supply-side onboarding, as the brief already plans.

## 3. Fractional coordination fee

- **AcreTrader formation fee** ~2.5% of $25K ticket = ~$625 per investor. For India equivalent ticket of ₹40L = ₹1 lakh per buyer.
- **Strata/hBits historical** ~2% upfront acquisition fee on ticket = ₹50K on ₹25L ticket.
- **Farmfolio Colombia FOA** — 0.5–1% annual, not upfront coordination.
- **Legal cost per partition in Karnataka** (lawyer + surveyor + revenue office) — ₹40–80K realistic for 4-buyer partition deed, phodi, mutation, individual khata creation.

**Recommended:**
- **Launch pricing: ₹50–60K per buyer** (₹2–2.5 lakh total on a 4-buyer, ₹1.5cr plot; 1.5% effective). Low enough to drive adoption on the first 5–10 pilot deals; covers legal + surveyor costs with modest margin.
- **Steady-state: ₹75–100K per buyer** (₹3–4 lakh total; 2–3% effective). Once process is templated and pilot case studies exist.
- **Deal-specific premium:** charge more (₹1–1.5L per buyer) for complex cases — 5+ buyers, PTCL-tainted chain, or large parcels requiring conversion.

## 4. Services revenue per deal

- **Registration** — stamp duty advisory + Kaveri booking + document drafting: ₹15–40K market rate; TruePlots in-brief range of ₹10–30K is validated.
- **Land conversion** (when required): Government fee ~₹25/sqmt + service fee ₹25–75K. TruePlots margin: ₹15–40K per conversion.
- **Construction design + farmhouse permissions:** 5–8% of construction cost typical architect fee; TruePlots as GC/referral: ₹50K–3L per project depending on scope.
- **Caretaker + maintenance:** ₹8K–25K per month ongoing; 15–20% margin on partner services.

**Fractional deals amplify services revenue 3–4× per acre** — directionally correct per brief. But caveat: if buyers acquire registration and construction services independently (not through TruePlots), the amplification doesn't show up in TruePlots revenue. Bundling discipline is key.

## 5. CAC for target segments

**Segment A (whole-plot ₹50L–₹2Cr):**
- Google Ads farmland Karnataka CPC: ₹35–120 per click (estimated from similar real-estate keywords).
- Lead-to-close conversion for high-intent SEO: ~1–3%.
- **Estimated CAC: ₹60,000–1.5L per closed deal.** Justifiable at 1.5% commission on ₹1cr deal = ₹1.5L revenue.

**Segment B (fractional ₹25–60L):**
- This is the hard case. If each of 4 buyers is acquired separately via paid:
  - 4 × ₹60K = ₹2.4L CAC per deal
  - Coordination fee revenue: ₹2–3L
  - Thin margin after legal/surveyor costs
- **If buyers come from affinity groups** (one lead introduces 2–3 others via WhatsApp, family, office network), effective per-buyer CAC drops to ₹15–25K.
- **Strategic implication:** fractional deal unit economics work ONLY if the acquisition model is affinity-group-based. Paid-search-acquired stranger pooling has AcreTrader-style unit economics — doesn't work.

**Segment C (portfolio investor ₹1Cr+):**
- Low-volume, high-touch; referral + direct outreach dominates. CAC effectively zero on 3rd+ deal per investor.

## 6. Reference unit economics — AcreTrader (as cautionary case)

- Raised **$60M Series B** in 2022.
- Placed $406M across 171 offerings by 2024.
- Acquired by Proterra August 2025 after 2 rounds of layoffs.
- Implied gross revenue on $406M at ~3.25% fee stack ≈ ~$13M over ~5 years. Insufficient to support $60M-funded headcount.
- **Takeaway:** even with scale, a retail fractional farmland crowdfunding platform with thin per-deal fees has structurally difficult unit economics. TruePlots must either (a) charge meaningfully higher than AcreTrader (possible with services bundling), or (b) run at lower cost structure than AcreTrader (possible with India dev/ops costs).
