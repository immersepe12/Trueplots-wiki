## 10. Competitive Landscape Scan

### 10.1 Direct Competitors — Same Customer, Same Problem

**No established trust-layer marketplace for farmland in South India at scale.** The closest direct competitor is **Farmland Bazaar** (farmlandbazaar.com), a 4-year-old marketplace aggregator claiming 50,000+ acres listed (June 2025 press, "Emerging PropTech Leader 2025"). Its business model is listing-fee + lead-referral aggregation across multiple developers. It has no visible verification layer, no escrow, no services layer, and no fractional product. Threat level: medium over 12 months (strong SEO/brand lead, thin product moat); high over 24 months if it adds verification — which is a straightforward partnership play.

**Newly identified direct competitor (correcting brief v0.2):**  **Saptashwa Jagati Farms** is actively operating in Kanakapura at April 2026 with a fractional slicing model — a 15-acre estate subdivided into ~600-sqyd slices at ₹45.74 lakh each, marketed with "Non-Profit-Sharing model ensuring complete ownership" language. This is a developer-led pre-subdivision pool (not stranger-formed post-sale pool), but the buyer outcome (individual title at ~₹40-50L ticket) is very close to what TruePlots proposes for Segment B. Multiple active 99acres listings confirm it is in-market. The brief's framing of "no one is doing fractional farmland in South India at scale" is empirically wrong at the developer-pool tier — though still accurate at the stranger-pool-post-sale tier.

### 10.2 Fractional / Co-Ownership Adjacent Players

**Indian fractional CRE (under SEBI SM REIT framework, March 2024):**
- **PropShare Investment Trust** — India's first SM REIT; 3 schemes launched (Platina ₹350+cr, Titania ₹473cr 1.61× subscribed, Celestia ₹244cr April 2026). Stock-exchange liquid exit.
- **hBits** — registered April 2025; ₹500cr AUM, 100K+ users; planning ₹400–500cr IPO.
- **Strata / Everstrat** — registered Jan 2025, **surrendered May 2025**; founder obtained anticipatory bail amid SEBI-impersonation allegations; Avigna Hosur builder dispute; SEBI formal investor caution. Cautionary tale.
- **WiseX (ex-Myre), Assetmonk, Aasthy** — not SM REIT registered; operating outside regulated perimeter.
- Demand signal: PropShare Platina SM REIT IPO subscribed only 9% initially — retail demand at ₹10L ticket is thin.

**These are NOT direct competitors** — their product (pooled units in a trust) is structurally different from TruePlots (individual titles). **SEBI SM REIT framework does NOT apply to individual-title joint purchases** (requires trust, units, pooling). TruePlots' real regulatory constraint is **SEBI CIS Regulations 1999**, not SM REIT.

**Indian fractional farmland (legal subdivision to individual titles at scale):** none found operating.

**Indian fractional farmland (SPV/LLP units):**
- **Growpital** (Zetta Farms) — **SHUT DOWN by SEBI April 2024** as unregistered CIS. ~₹184cr from 5,200 investors. Critical precedent for what NOT to do.
- **PACL** — historical (1996–2014), ₹60,000cr CIS, Supreme Court 2013; reason SEBI's radar remains hot.
- **FAAB FarmFraX** — SPV + CCD model, 2024 launch, operating under SM REIT assumption. Not a direct competitor.

**International analogs:**
- **Farmfolio (Colombia)** — the ONE international analog with individual-title fractional farmland. Tenants-in-common "LOT" structure + HOA-like Farm Owners Association. Most relevant operational template for TruePlots.
- **AcreTrader (US)** — SPV/LLC. $406M placed, 75 dispositions. **Acquired by Proterra August 2025** after 2 rounds of layoffs in 6 months. Retail crowdfunding unit economics failed.
- **FarmTogether, FarmFundr, Harvest Returns (US), MyFarm (NZ)** — all SPV/LP pooled vehicles, not individual titles.
- **No UK, Australian, or European platform** operates individual-title fractional farmland at scale.

### 10.4 Indirect Competitors — General Real Estate Marketplaces

List farmland as one category; no verification, no services.
- 99acres (only accessible from Phase 1 research: 13 agri listings in Kanakapura vs 162 residential = 12:1 ratio suggesting most farmland trades OFF these portals)
- MagicBricks, Housing.com, NoBroker, OLX/Quikr

### 10.5 Incumbents / Status Quo

- Informal broker networks (Mandya, Kanakapura, Doddaballapur, Hosur)
- Newspaper classifieds, WhatsApp groups, village-level walk-ins
- **Most farmland trade happens here, not on listed portals** — implied by the 12:1 residential-to-agricultural listing ratio on 99acres

### 10.6 Adjacent Services (Potential Partners or Competitors)

- Land lawyers and law firms (LegalKart, LawRato, local practitioners)
- **Licensed surveyors (CRITICAL partner for fractional product — needed for 11E sketch / phodi / mutation workflow)**
- Construction and design firms for farmhouses
- Managed farmland operators (potential partners for future managed product; potential direct competitors if they add marketplace breadth)

### 10.7 Regulatory Landscape Summary

| Regulation | Status April 2026 | Implication for TruePlots |
|---|---|---|
| Karnataka 79A/79B (non-agriculturist buyer restrictions) | **REPEALED since July 2020 ordinance** | Foundation of TruePlots TAM. Siddaramaiah govt has announced intent to restore but lacks Legislative Council numbers. **Monitor closely — reversal = TAM collapse.** |
| PTCL Act (SC/ST grantee land) | Amended 2024 (slightly loosened); still invokable on subsequent transfers | PTCL diligence must be core verification layer |
| Land subdivision (11E/phodi/mutation) | Operational; 3–12 month timeline | Subdivision is 3–9 month post-sale commitment per fractional deal |
| RERA | Explicitly excludes agricultural land | No RERA registration required or available |
| SEBI SM REIT | Does NOT apply to individual-title joint purchases | TruePlots is property-law, not securities-law |
| SEBI CIS Regulations 1999 | **Real regulatory risk** | Must architect: individual registered sale deeds, escrow-based money flow, no pooled fund, no managed yield promise. Growpital is the live precedent (₹184cr, shut down April 2024). |
