# Urban Sites — Pricing & Unit Economics Benchmarks

*Format matches parent [05-pricing-benchmarks.md](../05-pricing-benchmarks.md). Urban-specific benchmarks with differences from farmland called out.*

## Summary comparison to farmland

| Metric | Farmland benchmark | Urban benchmark | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Transaction commission | 1.5–2.5% seller-side | 1–2% each side (Bangalore norm 2%+2% on plots) | **Competitive pressure on urban take rate — NoBroker zero-brokerage positioning forces TruePlots lower** |
| Legal verification cost | ₹5–12K (Bhoomi + EC + KGIS) | ₹10–25K standard; up to ₹1L complex | **Higher cost absorbed per deal; ticket size also higher — margin neutral** |
| Subdivision operational cost | ₹40–80K (11E + phodi + mutation) | ₹5–15K (khata bifurcation) + partition deed ~1% stamp duty | **Urban subdivision cheaper in cash but regulatory timeline similar (3–6 months)** |
| Stamp duty + registration | 5% stamp + 2% registration = ~7% (Karnataka, >₹45L) | Same | Same — both took the 2% registration hit in Aug 2025 |
| CAC | ₹60K–1.5L (Segment A); affinity-group dependent | **CPC ₹57–290; CPL ₹3–6K high-intent**; realistic CAC ₹40K–1L | **Urban CAC likely 30–50% lower than farmland due to denser search volume, BUT conversion rates lower due to competitive density** |
| Deal cycle | 3–6 months (pre-fractional); 6–12 months (fractional full flow) | 3–6 months (solo); 6+ months (fractional) | Similar magnitude |

## 1. Transaction commission

- **Bangalore broker norm:** 1–2% each side; individual plots often see 2% buyer + 2% seller (total 4%). This is higher than farmland norms.
- **NoBroker zero-brokerage** positioning competes directly against this — they earn from owner packs + upsells, not transaction fees.
- **TruePlots urban take rate must navigate this.** Leading with 2% transaction fee in a market where NoBroker positions "zero" is a branding challenge.

**Recommended:**
- **1.0–1.5% of sale value, seller-side only.** Lower than farmland (1.5–2.5%) specifically because of NoBroker pressure. Compensate margin via verification fee + coordination fee + services bundling.
- **Cap at ₹2L on deals over ₹1.5cr** — competitive with sophisticated buyer expectations.

## 2. Listing / verification fees

- **Legal-grade verification cost to TruePlots per listing:** ₹10–20K (BBMP khata audit + encumbrance + layout approval check + RERA compliance + title chain)
- **NoBroker's advocate-verification fee to buyer:** ~₹10–25K per property
- **Independent Bangalore lawyer plot verification:** ₹5–25K standard; ₹30K+ comprehensive

**Recommended:**
- **₹8–12K listing fee to seller/broker** (similar to 99acres/MagicBricks premium listing, covers internal verification cost)
- **₹20–25K standalone buyer-side verification report** (competitive with NoBroker advocate service but deeper — includes RERA check, khata status, bifurcation feasibility)

## 3. Fractional coordination fee (urban-specific)

- **Khata bifurcation commodity cost:** ₹5–15K per bifurcation
- **Partition deed stamp duty:** ~1% of property value
- **Surveyor + BBMP approval + legal drafting (2-buyer split of 60×40):** ~₹30–50K all-in
- **Benchmark vs farmland:** urban subdivision is cheaper and simpler because (a) plots are smaller, (b) BBMP process is more templated than Tahsildar farmland phodi, (c) RERA adds complexity but also standardization

**Recommended:**
- **Launch pricing: ₹40–50K per buyer** (₹80K–1L total on 2-buyer split of ₹1cr plot; ~1% effective). Lower than farmland (₹50–60K) because urban is more cost-sensitive and NoBroker-anchored.
- **Steady-state: ₹60–75K per buyer** once case studies and BBMP relationships mature.
- **Complex cases** (B-khata → A-khata upgrade, 3-buyer split, non-standard BBMP jurisdictions): ₹1L+ per buyer — buyer pays for the additional legal complexity.

## 4. Services revenue per urban deal

- **Registration support** (stamp duty advisory + Kaveri + document drafting): ₹15–30K market rate
- **Khata transfer (post-purchase):** ₹5–15K for A-khata; B→A conversion regularisation fee if applicable
- **Home loan facilitation** (for A-khata plots): 0.5–1% referral commission (₹50K–1L on ₹1cr loan)
- **Construction management** (if plot buyer builds house): 8–12% of construction cost; ₹3–6L on a ₹40L build — but only a subset of buyers build immediately
- **Home loan is a new services line vs farmland** (farmland generally not loan-eligible; urban A-khata is)

**Fractional services amplification** (2-buyer deal vs 1-buyer):
- 2× registration, 2× khata transfer, 2× home loan referrals (if applicable)
- ~1.5–2× services revenue per acre — **smaller amplification than farmland** (where 4-buyer deals were common); urban typically caps at 2–3 buyers due to plot-size constraints

## 5. CAC for urban segments

**Segment A (solo urban plot buyer, ₹60L–₹2Cr):**
- Google Ads CPC ₹57–290; plot-specific long-tail probably mid-range ₹100–150
- Lead-to-close ~1–3%
- **Estimated CAC: ₹40K–1L per closed deal.** Lower than farmland due to denser search volume; but conversion rates lower due to NoBroker/99acres competition.

**Segment B (fractional urban buyer, ₹40–60L):**
- If each of 2 buyers acquired independently via paid: 2 × ₹50K = ₹1L CAC per deal
- Coordination fee revenue: ₹80K–1L
- **Thin margin unless affinity-group acquisition works** — same structural risk as farmland but magnified because urban plots attract less "group purchase with friends" behavior (cultural norm: urban plots bought solo or with spouse; farmland more friend-group natural)
- **This is a material risk:** urban Segment B unit economics are harder than farmland Segment B

**Segment C (NRI urban investor, ₹1.5cr+):**
- Referral + direct outreach dominates. Effectively zero CAC on 2nd+ deal.

## 6. Key differences from farmland economics

1. **Take rate compression** — NoBroker's zero-brokerage forces TruePlots to 1–1.5% vs farmland's 1.5–2.5%.
2. **Home loan opportunity** — A-khata urban plots are bankable; adds ~₹50K–1L services revenue per deal not available in farmland.
3. **Shorter deal cycle theoretically** — but competitive density (more buyers shopping more plots on more portals) extends discovery time in practice.
4. **Fractional amplification smaller** — typically 2-buyer urban splits vs 3-4-buyer farmland pools; services revenue amplification is 2× not 4×.
5. **RERA compliance cost** — ongoing regulatory obligation absent in farmland; needs dedicated compliance resource starting month 1, not month 6.
