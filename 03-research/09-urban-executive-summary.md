# Urban Sites Top-Up — Executive Summary

*1-page synthesis. Three decisions.*

---

## 1. Is urban sites genuine whitespace for the fractional model, or competitive mud?

**Whitespace — confirmed empirically.** No Indian player operates matchmaking + verification + joint-sale + subdivision-to-individual-titles as a single product for urban residential plots. Adjacent players serve adjacent problems:

- **Fractional CRE** (Strata, hBits, PropShare) — commercial only, SPV/unit structure, no individual titles
- **Khata bifurcation services** (Khata Broker, EzyLegal, Bino.bot) — post-facto paper-filers, no matchmaking
- **Horizontal marketplaces** (99acres, MagicBricks, NoBroker, Square Yards) — zero fractional products today
- **Layout developers** (Prestige, Brigade, NBR Group) — first-sale solo only

Latent demand is visible on Kaanoon, Quora, and Reddit — buyers asking exactly TruePlots' question with no productized answer.

**BUT the defensibility window is narrower than farmland**: 9–12 months vs farmland's 18. The binding constraint is NoBroker — they already have legal verification as a paid upsell, 6,768 Bangalore plot listings, product velocity, and capital. They can ship "verified + co-buy" in one quarter if TruePlots gains traction.

## 2. What's different about executing in urban vs farmland?

**Harder on three dimensions:**

- **Fast-follow velocity.** Farmland threats (Hasiru, Farmland Bazaar) are slow-moving. Urban threat (NoBroker) is 1 quarter from parity.
- **Regulatory surface.** Karnataka RERA applies to plotted developments >500 sqm / >8 units (farmland is RERA-exempt). Facilitator-vs-promoter classification is unresolved and unit-economics-critical — needs specialist counsel memo before first deal.
- **B-Khata financing constraint.** ~30–50% of Bangalore peripheral plot inventory is B-Khata; those plots can't get mainstream home loans, breaking the fractional affordability pitch for financing-dependent buyers.

**Easier on three dimensions:**

- **Shorter deal cycle.** 3–6 months vs 3–9 months farmland.
- **Lower subdivision cost.** Khata bifurcation (₹5–15K) is simpler than farmland phodi/mutation (₹40–80K).
- **Higher buyer intent.** Google Ads for "buy plot Bangalore" has better conversion than farmland searches; better CAC economics.

**Similar per-deal economics.** Net contribution per fractional deal ~₹3–4L in affinity-group acquisition case. Turns loss-making if stranger pooling + paid acquisition × 3 buyers.

## 3. Should TruePlots proceed with month-4 urban launch, delay, or deprioritize?

**Recommendation: Conditional proceed. Launch month 4 IF three gates are met; else delay to month 6–9.**

### Gates for month-4 launch

1. **First 5 farmland fractional deals are complete with clean individual khatas.** Operational templates, surveyor relationships, and escrow architecture are battle-tested, not theoretical. If farmland hasn't closed 5 clean deals by month 3, delaying urban preserves founder focus.
2. **Karnataka RERA specialist counsel memo is in hand.** Written opinion on facilitator-vs-promoter classification of the 3-buyer urban subdivision model, with structural recommendations for staying facilitator. If this is not secured by month 3, legal exposure on urban is too high to launch.
3. **A-Khata inventory validation.** Mystery-shop 3 BMRDA layout developers in different corridors; confirm >100 A-Khata plots in ₹40–75L range available per month across target Bangalore belts. If A-Khata inventory is tighter than assumed, TAM is too constrained for the fractional model to reach 20+ deals in 9 months.

### If gates are met — launch with these constraints

- **A-Khata plots only** at launch. B-Khata comes later as a B→A regularization service SKU.
- **9-month target of 20+ completed urban fractional deals** with individual khatas. That's the defensibility flip: "only one doing it" → "most operationally mature." Miss this and NoBroker's copy ships to a commodity market.
- **Moat = operational stack, not marketplace UI.** BBMP/BMRDA khata bifurcation playbook, lawyer templates, surveyor network, 3-buyer coordination flow. Compound with every deal.
- **Supply-side partnership strategy for BMRDA layout developers** (NBR Group, Oraiyan, Vaishnavi) with stuck unsold 60×40+ inventory. Urban analog of "stuck large farmland plots" thesis.

### If gates are NOT met — delay urban to month 6–9

Farmland is the hero brand. Urban is capability extension. Launching urban before farmland is proven fragments founder attention across two unproven verticals — classic Indian proptech failure mode. Better to be the category-defining farmland fractional platform at month 9 than to be the second-best at both at month 12.

---

## Bottom line

Urban fractional is real whitespace with tighter execution pressure. Proceed if farmland has proven the model. Delay if it hasn't. The NoBroker fast-follow clock starts the day TruePlots visibly launches urban — make sure the operational stack is already in place when it does.
