# Urban Sites — Top Blind Spots

*Three things the founder is probably getting wrong about the urban sites thesis. Severity-ranked.*

---

## 1. NoBroker is not Farmland Bazaar — they will ship faster than TruePlots

**Likely framing in founder's head:** *"Urban sites is just farmland applied to Bangalore plots. Same verification + fractional playbook works."*

**Reality:** The urban competitive landscape is categorically different from farmland.

In farmland, the incumbent threat was **Farmland Bazaar** — a 4-year-old React SPA with 50K acres claimed but zero verification layer, zero escrow, zero fractional. Weak product, strong SEO. TruePlots could plausibly out-execute them on 3-deal volume in a quarter.

In urban, the incumbent threat is **NoBroker** — Tiger Global / Accel / General Atlantic-funded, 6,768 Bangalore plot listings live, **advocate-based legal verification already in product as a paid upsell**, zero-brokerage brand narrative already aligned with disintermediation. If TruePlots' first 10 urban fractional deals make press, NoBroker engineers ship a co-buy MVP in one quarter. They have every ingredient except the buyer-matching mechanic — which is the easiest piece to build (a waitlist + simple compatibility filters).

**What this means concretely:**
- Farmland defensibility window: 12–18 months. Urban: **6–12 months**.
- Farmland moat includes verification novelty. Urban: **verification is table stakes — NoBroker already sells it.**
- Farmland launch timeline (month 3–6 MVP + month 6–12 fractional productization) had breathing room. Urban **month-4 launch with fractional as afterthought leaves NoBroker room to copy before TruePlots has 20 completed deals.**

**What to do:**
- Either enter urban **hard and fast** (20+ fractional deals in 9 months, specialist brand lock-in, operational depth on BBMP khata bifurcation) — treat it as a priority-1 vertical, not a capability extension
- Or **delay urban launch to month 9–12** until farmland execution is templated and the team can commit full attention
- Do NOT launch urban at month 4 as a "second vertical" with 40% of team attention. That's the scenario where NoBroker eats your lunch.

## 2. Verification is not the urban positioning — fractional is

**Likely framing in founder's head:** *"TruePlots' brand promise is 'verified before you see it.' Apply it to urban and win trust."*

**Reality:** Every major urban portal already has some verification layer.

- 99acres: self-declared "verified" badge (marketing-grade)
- MagicBricks: "verified owner" self-cert
- Homes247: "verified and handpicked"
- **NoBroker: actual advocate-based legal verification as paid upsell (₹10–25K)**

The farmland verification moat that works — Bhoomi + KGIS + PTCL + 79A/B applicability depth — does not transfer. Urban verification is BBMP khata audit + layout approval + RERA compliance + encumbrance, all of which any incumbent can partner-build in weeks with 1 lawyer partnership. "Every urban plot verified" is NOT a differentiated claim to an urban buyer — they've seen 4 "verified" badges already today.

**The urban wedge is fractional.** No one else does buyer-matching + joint purchase + individual-khata subdivision. Positioning must lead with that:

- Not "every urban plot verified" (crowded claim, weak moat)
- But "own the 60×40 you can't afford — split it legally with a verified co-buyer" (novel claim, stronger moat)

Section 9 of brief v0.2 (brand direction) uses "Verified" as the signature word. For urban extension, that word choice is wrong. "Split," "Share-with-title," "Co-own," or similar is the urban hero word.

**What to do:**
- Develop urban-specific brand language before launch, not during
- Rename or sub-brand urban offering if needed (*"TruePlots Split"* / *"TruePlots Share"*) to avoid brand dilution from farmland
- Run 3 urban-specific A/B marketing tests — verification-led vs fractional-led — before committing to a landing page

## 3. RERA will decide whether month-4 launch is even legal

**Likely framing in founder's head:** *"RERA is for developers selling apartments. We're a facilitator — it doesn't apply to us."*

**Reality:** Karnataka RERA requires registration for **plotted developments >500 sqm or >8 units.** That includes plot sales with a "promoter" role. The question of whether TruePlots is a facilitator (needs only agent registration) or a promoter (needs project registration) is a legal judgment call, not a self-classification.

Structural features that push TruePlots toward **facilitator** (RERA light):
- TruePlots does not own the plot
- Buyers sign sale deeds directly with the seller
- TruePlots earns coordination/verification fees, not development margin
- Money flows buyer → seller via escrow, not through TruePlots balance sheet

Structural features that push TruePlots toward **promoter** (RERA project registration, disclosure, allottee consent rules):
- TruePlots markets the subdivision plan to buyers
- TruePlots coordinates the sale from multiple buyers to one seller
- TruePlots provides the subdivision design and legal template
- In the BBMP khata bifurcation, one could argue TruePlots is "developing" the plot into 2-3 sub-plots

The line is not clear from a pure reading of RERA. Karnataka RERA has issued orders on facilitator-promoter distinction for plotted developments that go both ways depending on marketing framing and money flow.

**If TruePlots is classified as promoter:**
- Project registration required for every 3+ buyer deal on a >500 sqm plot (most TruePlots deals)
- Disclosure obligations: layout plan, approvals, promoter details
- 2/3 allottee consent for any plan modification
- Timelines, financial covenants, escrow requirements — all apply
- **Month-4 launch is not possible** — RERA registration takes 3–6 months per project

**What to do, URGENTLY:**
- **Get a written counsel opinion on facilitator-vs-promoter classification BEFORE committing to month-4 launch.** Not after the first deal. Before.
- Structure the earliest pilot deals to maximize facilitator indicators (buyer signs with seller, money via escrow, TruePlots earns fees not margin)
- Build RERA-compliance capability (even if just agent registration) as a month-1 priority, not month-6
- If counsel opinion is ambiguous, consider filing a pre-emptive KRERA clarification request — better to know than to be raided

This is the single operational risk that could turn a month-4 launch into a SEBI-style Growpital disaster. Don't hand-wave it.
