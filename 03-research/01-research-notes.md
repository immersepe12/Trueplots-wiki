## Part A — Indian fractional farmland with individual titles

### The headline finding

**The brief's "whitespace" claim holds up.** No Indian platform has operated at scale where strangers jointly acquire one farmland parcel and each receives an individual registered sale deed + khata after post-sale subdivision.

Every Indian player found falls into one of three structurally different models:

1. **SPV/LLP fractional-share** (Growpital, PACL, FAAB FarmFraX) — buyers hold units in a holding entity, not land title.
2. **Managed farmland developers** (Hosachiguru, Beforest, Swasya, Sanjeevani, Sanctity Ferme, etc.) — developer pre-subdivides on its own balance sheet and sells one-buyer-one-plot.
3. **Community farming / rent-a-plot** (Farmizen) — leases, not ownership.

### Growpital — the load-bearing precedent

- Founded 2020 as Zetta Farms Private Limited. 11–14% "tax-free assured returns." ~₹184 cr raised from 5,200+ investor-partners via LLP veneer.
- **SEBI interim order 29 Jan 2024, confirmed April 2024**: unregistered Collective Investment Scheme disguised as an LLP. Banned from securities markets. ([Inc42](https://inc42.com/features/how-growpitals-illegal-investment-model-left-5k-investor-partners-in-the-lurch/), [Business Standard](https://www.business-standard.com/companies/news/sebi-bars-growpital-other-related-entities-from-securities-markets-124042800454_1.html))
- **Failure mode:** regulatory. SEBI view: pooled public money + promised returns + professional management = CIS, regardless of LLP wrapper.
- **Lesson:** Individual-title structure is the cleanest escape from CIS classification. Buyers must each sign registered sale deeds in their own names. Money flow must be buyer → seller (via escrow), not buyer → TruePlots → seller.

### PACL — the reason SEBI's CIS radar stays hot

- Nirmal Singh Bhangoo, 1996–2014. Rs 60,000 cr collected from 5 crore investors.
- Supreme Court Feb 2013: CIS. SEBI refunded Rs 1,021 cr to 20.8 lakh investors as of 2022. ([SEBI PACL Portal](https://www.sebi.gov.in/PACL.html))
- Only 19,284 of millions got registered sale deeds — the rest held paper allotment letters. Classic Ponzi.
- **Lesson:** every farmland scheme in India is now presumed-CIS until proven otherwise.

### FAAB FarmFraX — current operator of the SPV model

- Launched 2024. Pvt Ltd SPV holds title; investors receive 1 equity share + 9 CCDs per unit. 7% rental yield projected, 16.2% IRR. 2% p.a. AMC + 2% exit. ([The Alt Investor](https://blog.thealtinvestor.in/faab-farmfrax-a-fractional-agricultural-land-opportunity))
- Operating under assumption SM REIT will eventually be extended to farmland (not currently applicable).
- **Lesson:** This is the "safe but different" play. Not a TruePlots competitor because investors own shares, not land.

### Karnataka regulatory environment (focused)

**Section 79A / 79B — REPEALED since 2020 Amendment, politically contested**
- BJP govt (Bommai/Yediyurappa) repealed Sections 79A, 79B, 79C via ordinance 13 July 2020. Non-agriculturists (any Indian resident, company, trust, LLP) can buy farmland in Karnataka without the earlier ₹25L non-agri-income cap.
- Siddaramaiah Congress govt has publicly announced intent to restore but has not secured Legislative Council numbers as of 2024–2025.
- **As of April 2026, 79A/79B remain repealed.** This is the foundation of TruePlots' TAM — a reversal sharply constrains the non-farmer buyer segment. ([The Hindu 2024](https://www.thehindu.com/news/national/karnataka/govt-set-to-bring-back-provisions-in-land-reforms-act-that-bar-non-farmers-from-buying-land/article68547055.ece))

**PTCL Act — amended 2024, slightly loosened**
- SC/ST (Prohibition of Transfer of Certain Lands) Act 1978 voids sales of land originally granted to SC/ST grantees without Govt permission.
- **2024 amendment** removed NOC requirement for some sales; Nekkanti Rama Lakshmi SC ruling says proceedings must be initiated within "reasonable time" — 25-year delays unreasonable.
- But PTCL can still be invoked on subsequent transfers; sales violating specific govt permission conditions remain void.
- **PTCL diligence must be in every TruePlots listing's verification stack.** ([SCC Online Blog 2024](https://www.scconline.com/blog/post/2024/09/13/karnataka-sc-st-prohibition-transfer-certain-lands-amendment-act/))

**Subdivision process (critical for TruePlots fractional product)**
- Partition deed registered → file phodi/mutation under Karnataka Land Revenue Act → Assistant Director of Land Records issues new hissa numbers with 11E sketch → each new hissa gets own RTC and khata.
- **Timeline:** 3–6 months clean cases; 6–12+ months with objections or backlog.
- **Minimum extent rules**: Karnataka Prevention of Fragmentation Act sets minimum holdings (1 acre irrigated, 2 acres dry typical); subdivision below threshold needs Tahsildar exemption.
- **The subdivision-to-title promise is a 3–9 month post-sale operational commitment.** ([Hasiru Farms 11E/Phodi guide](https://hasirufarms.com/blogs/karnataka-11e-phodi-mutation))

**RERA** — explicitly excludes agricultural land. No RERA registration required or available for pure farmland.

## Part B — International analogs

### Structural dichotomy

Virtually every international farmland platform issues **shares in an SPV/LLC/LP/REIT**, not individual land titles. TruePlots' individual-title model is rare globally.

### AcreTrader (US) — the cautionary tale

- SPV/LLC per farm; investors buy LLC membership interests. Reg D Rule 506(c), accredited only. ~$10K min, $12K–25K typical.
- Fees: ~2.5% formation + 0.75% annual + 5% disposition.
- **$406M placed, 171 offerings, ~39,000 acres, 75 dispositions with 9.4%–30.3% net IRR** as of 2024–2025. ([PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/acretrader-delivers-over-135m-in-investor-returns-from-farmland-dispositions-302727600.html))
- **2024 troubles:** two rounds of layoffs in 6 months, halted new offerings for months, almond farm bankruptcy, distribution timing complaints.
- **Aug 2025:** Acquired by Proterra Investment Partners. Pivoting from retail crowdfunding to institutional. ([Arkansas Business](https://www.arkansasbusiness.com/article/acretrader-acquired-by-proterra-investment-partners/))
- **Lesson:** Even the best-funded US retail farmland crowdfunding ($60M Series B 2022) could not reach stable unit economics. Retail CAC and distribution timing broke the model.

### FarmTogether (US)

- SPV model. Reg D 506(c). $15K min crowdfunded, $50K sustainable fund, $3M+ bespoke. Fee stack: ~2% upfront + 1–2% annual + NOI share + carry — heavy.
- Founder transitioned out mid-2023; pivoting toward institutional. Still operational — closed $6.6M citrus grove Oct 2025.

### FarmFundr, Harvest Returns, Steward (US)

- FarmFundr: LLC per deal, Reg D/A+/CF, $10K–100K, California specialty crops only. Small scale.
- Harvest Returns: LP per deal. **Sponsor-pays model — no investor fees.** ~18K investors, broader ag scope.
- Steward: Agricultural lending, not equity. $100 minimum. Not a TruePlots analog (debt vs equity).

### Rural Funds Group (Australia) / MyFarm + Syndex (NZ)

- RFF: ASX-listed REIT, ~A$2.4B agricultural AUM. Public shares, not fractional titles.
- MyFarm: LP syndicates, ~NZ$550M AUM, 300 investors since 1990. **Syndex platform** is the most developed secondary market for agri-syndicates globally — worth studying for TruePlots exit design.

### Farmfolio (Colombia) — the ONE individual-title analog

- **Tenancy-in-common via "LOTs"** (Land Ownership Titles). Farmfolio subdivides larger farms and sells demarcated parcels to investors with individual title. Farm Owners Association (FOA, HOA-like) manages operations at 0.5–1% annual fee. ($30K–60K minimum.) ([Farmfolio](https://farmfolio.net/how-does-it-work))
- **This is the most relevant operational template TruePlots has internationally.** Specifically for FOA governance, dispute resolution, fee collection mechanics.

### What does NOT exist internationally

- No large-scale individual-title fractional farmland platform in US, UK, Australia, or Europe.
- Farmfolio (Colombia) is the only recognizable precedent — small scale, specialty crops.
- **The India-specific combination appears structurally unique.** Probably enabled (or forced) by India's state-level agricultural land laws that restrict corporate ownership, pushing toward individual rather than SPV structures.

## Part C — Indian fractional commercial RE + SEBI SM REIT framework

### Does SM REIT reach TruePlots' individual-title joint purchases? **No.**

SEBI (REIT) (Amendment) Regulations 2024 Chapter VIB requires:
- Trust under Indian Trusts Act
- Pooled funds from 20+ investors
- Units of minimum ₹10 lakh
- Asset size ₹50–500 cr
- 100% equity of SPVs, SPVs as Companies Act entities
- 95% assets in completed revenue-generating real estate

**SM REIT is inherently a pooled investment-trust with unitholders holding beneficial interest through units.** Property-law joint purchase + partition + individual khatas is not pooling, not units, not securities — it's outside SEBI's domain. ([Nishith Desai FOP policy paper](https://www.nishithdesai.com/fileadmin/user_upload/pdfs/Policy-Paper/Governance_of_Fractional_Ownership_Platforms.pdf))

### The real regulatory risk: CIS Regulations 1999

Section 11AA four-factor test for a CIS:
1. Contributions pooled
2. Pooled for profits/income
3. Day-to-day management by person other than contributor
4. Contributor has no control over management

**SEBI has previously held joint-ownership/partnership wrappers can still be CIS** if economic character matches. Growpital is the live precedent. ([Vinod Kothari analysis](https://vinodkothari.com/2023/09/fractional-ownership-schemes-distinguishing-between-investment-schemes-and-shared-ownership-of-real-assets/))

**TruePlots stays outside CIS if:** each buyer individually signs registered sale deed; money flows buyer → seller via escrow (not through TruePlots balance sheet); no promised/implied returns; no sponsor-managed profit-sharing scheme; TruePlots earns fees for facilitation, not yield.

**TruePlots gets dragged into CIS if:** marketing leads with "returns" or "appreciation"; any pooled fund or SPV holds title; TruePlots manages the asset post-sale for shared profit.

### Fractional CRE players — current status

| Platform | SM REIT status | Ticket | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **PropShare (Property Share)** | REGISTERED — India's FIRST SM REIT | ₹10L | 3 schemes: Platina (subscribed 9% initially, extended), Titania (1.61x), Celestia (Apr 2026). Stock-exchange liquidity. |

### Demand signal

- PropShare Platina SM REIT IPO **subscribed only 9% initially** — retail demand at ₹10L ticket is thin.
- Combined fractional CRE AUM <₹3,000 cr across all players in 6 years — tiny vs mutual funds/PMS.
- **Bet-implication for TruePlots:** Land (culturally understood, individual title) at lower tickets may match Indian retail psychology better than the SM REIT "units in a trust" framing.

## Part E — Large-plot inventory signal (99acres Kanakapura)

*Research environment blocked MagicBricks, Housing.com, Reddit, YouTube, Google search. 99acres was the only accessible source.*

### The 13-listing Kanakapura agricultural inventory (April 2026)

| Size band | Count | % of total | Observed age |
|---|---|---|---|
| ≥5 acres | 2 | 15% | 2–4 months |
| 2–5 acres | 4 | 31% | mixed |
| <2 acres | 7 | 54% | 1 week – 1 month |

**Compare to residential plots same locality:** 162 listings vs 13 agricultural = **12:1 ratio** suggesting most farmland trades off 99acres (broker networks, WhatsApp, village).

### Specific signals for the "stuck large plot" thesis

**Supporting:**
- Kodihalli 5.5 ac owner-listed, **4 months old** (no broker).
- Mullalli 8 ac at **₹60L/acre vs ₹1.2Cr/acre comps** — ~50% discount implied, owner-listed.
- Gerahalli 2 ac sat 5 months (owner).

**Against — and this is the critical finding:**
- **Saptashwa Jagati Farms** is selling fractional slices of a 15-acre Kanakapura estate at **₹45.74L per ~600 sqyd slice** ("non-profit-sharing model ensuring complete ownership"). **This is a dealer already running the exact pool-to-buy-big product TruePlots proposes**, in TruePlots' exact target geography.
- 54% of listings are sub-2-acre — market has already fragmented itself.
- Residential converted plots dominate 12:1.

### Kanakapura price bands (April 2026, from 99acres)

- **Raw agricultural:** ₹85L–₹1.7Cr/acre, median ~₹1.2Cr
- **Managed fractional slice (Saptashwa):** ~₹3.5Cr/acre effective (2–3× raw)
- **Residential converted:** ~₹12Cr/acre equivalent (7–10× raw)

### Verdict on thesis

- **"Stuck inventory" thesis: weakly/partially validated.** 2 of 13 large, one 4-month-old, one discounted ~50% — real but modest signal.
- **"Unserved ₹25–50L demand" thesis: unverified from this session** (forums blocked) BUT Saptashwa's ₹45.74L SKU actively marketed by a Dealer is commercial evidence the price point has sellable demand.
- **Strategic implication:** Bottleneck is not "large plots can't find buyers" — it's "only developer-led pools currently convert them, at 2–3× premium." TruePlots' edge must be **lower-friction/lower-margin pooling** than Saptashwa-style developer-pools.
- **Brief's "no one does fractional in South India" framing is contradicted by 99acres listings in the target city.** This is a blindspot to correct.
