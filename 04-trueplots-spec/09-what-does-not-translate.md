# What Does NOT Translate — Spinny Features TruePlots Should NOT Copy

*Explicit list. If anyone on the team proposes these patterns, point them here.*

---

## 1. Buy-and-hold inventory model (CRITICAL)

**Spinny does:** Buys cars from sellers, refurbishes at Spinny hubs, adds margin, sells to buyers. Full-stack D2C. Carries working capital tied up in inventory.

**Why TruePlots must not copy:** The project brief explicitly positions TruePlots as a **facilitator**, not an inventory-holder. Reasons this matters:
- **Capital efficiency** — buying plots to resell requires ₹100cr+ of working capital per city to build meaningful inventory. TruePlots' competitive advantage is capital-light scale.
- **Regulatory exposure** — owning + reselling plots at margin transforms TruePlots from facilitator to RERA promoter, triggering project registration per layout. Completely different unit economics.
- **Financing structure** — TruePlots' 1.5-2.5% transaction commission model doesn't work if you're also absorbing ₹1-2Cr of plot cost per deal pending resale. Cash conversion cycle becomes months, not days.
- **CIS risk** — holding pooled plots on balance sheet pending fractional allotment edges into Collective Investment Scheme territory (Growpital precedent).

**If anyone proposes:** "Let's acquire a few stuck parcels directly to seed fractional supply" — reject. The right move is operating as deal facilitator on third-party inventory, with seller-side escrow arrangements if supply-side capital is needed.

---

## 2. Fast-purchase decision cycle

**Spinny does:** Buyer browses → books test drive → decides → pays → receives car in days. Typical end-to-end cycle: 7-14 days.

**Why TruePlots must not copy:** Plot buying is a months-long process. Force-compressing it creates:
- **Shallow verification** — proper 120-point verification takes 3-7 days per plot; trying to fit this into a 48-hour funnel means cutting corners
- **Regulatory impossibility** — Karnataka stamp duty + Kaveri slot booking + khata transfer cannot be compressed below 4-6 weeks
- **Buyer-side regret** — high-value illiquid purchases should feel considered, not fast. Compressing creates post-purchase anxiety that feeds into the 7-day refund window and damages trust.

**What to do instead:** Design the UX for a 4-8 week decision cycle. Use the time for buyer education and trust reinforcement (WhatsApp nurture, Q&A call cadence, additional verification deep-dive on request).

---

## 3. Volume-driven advertising

**Spinny does:** Big-budget TV campaigns with Sachin Tendulkar ("Khushiyon ki Long Drive," "Go Far"). YouTube pre-rolls. Outdoor. Akshay Tritiya 800-car single-day activation.

**Why TruePlots must not copy initially:** Plot buyers are low-volume, high-consideration. Mass TV/YouTube CAC is wasteful at early stage.

**What works instead (at least for years 1-3):**
- SEO content strategy (per project brief §11.2)
- Founder-led content (LinkedIn, YouTube)
- WhatsApp-first lead capture (matches South Indian buyer behavior)
- Referral loops (one satisfied buyer introduces 2-3 more)
- PR via verified-deal case studies

**Reconsider mass advertising at:** 200+ completed deals, ₹50cr+ annual GMV, and clear channel economics. Not before.

---

## 4. Spinny Buyback / guaranteed resale value

**Spinny does:** Offers guaranteed repurchase at pre-committed price for cars owned 6-18 months.

**Why TruePlots must not copy:** Requires either (a) TruePlots holding capital to repurchase (conflicts with facilitator model), or (b) a committed institutional buyer for plots (doesn't exist at TruePlots scale). Plots appreciate over years, not months — the economics of guaranteed buyback are structurally different.

**Alternative:** Offer "TruePlots Resale Assist" — a brokerage-style service that finds buyers on commission when owners want to exit. Clearly not a guarantee. This is honest and scalable.

---

## 5. Aggressive financing push

**Spinny does:** Moderately prominent financing — EMI anchor on cards, inline calculator. But not forced.

**Caution for TruePlots:** Cars24 has MORE aggressive finance push, and Spinny deliberately doesn't. For plots:
- Aggressive "finance-first" push before verification trust is established seems desperate
- Plot loans are slower (days/weeks, not hours) — misleading buyers on speed creates disappointment
- B-Khata exclusion is a hard constraint — if TruePlots advertises "finance any plot," buyers land on listings where financing is impossible

**What to do:** Follow Spinny's moderate-not-aggressive pattern. EMI anchor on cards, inline calculator, soft pre-approval CTA. Don't gate buyer browsing behind finance pre-approval.

---

## 6. Celebrity endorsement (at early stage)

**Spinny does:** Sachin Tendulkar is the face across TVC/YouTube.

**Caution for TruePlots:** Tendulkar is a $50M+ deal over 5 years, signed only after Spinny had Series E traction ($283M, $1.8B valuation). Copying this pattern pre-traction signals:
- Capital allocation misjudgment
- Brand trying to buy legitimacy rather than earn it
- Founder distraction from core operations

**When to reconsider:** Post 500+ completed deals and clear category leadership position. Not before.

---

## 7. 57-hub physical footprint

**Spinny does:** 57 car hubs across 22 cities. Each hub is a ~2-acre facility with inventory storage, inspection bays, refurbishment, offices.

**Why TruePlots must not copy:** Plots don't need physical infrastructure. Buyers visit plots, not TruePlots hubs. A "TruePlots office in Koramangala" doesn't drive transactions the way a Spinny hub does.

**What TruePlots needs instead:**
- Regional ops teams (2-3 person ops pods per city) — not a hub
- Verification team (distributed, remote-first) — not in a physical office
- Relationship Managers (field-ops + some in-person but mostly WhatsApp/phone)

Avoid real estate CapEx until v3+.

---

## 8. "300-point inspection" escalation

**Cars24 claim:** "300-point inspection" (vs Spinny's 200). Inspection number inflation is a race to the bottom.

**Why TruePlots must not copy:** Picking "200 points" or "500 points" for TruePlots based on what sounds bigger than a competitor is a trap. 120 checkpoints was chosen because it maps to real Karnataka revenue-department reality, not because it sounds impressive. Do not inflate the number to "beat" a future competitor — the number should reflect what's actually being checked.

If asked by marketing "why not 200 like Spinny?": because Karnataka revenue records don't require 200 checks; 120 is the honest number.

---

## 9. Spinny360 as-is

**Spinny does:** Interactive 360° car walkthrough (gallery + interior rotation) in app.

**Partial translation only:** Plots are not cars — you can't rotate a plot. What translates:
- **Drone aerial walkthrough** (for farmland) — useful and differentiating
- **Ground-level video tour** (for urban plots) — match listing quality

**What does not translate:**
- Interior rotation UI
- 360° immersive view as the default visual paradigm

Plots benefit more from map overlay + drone photography than 360° immersive. Different visual vocabulary.

---

## 10. Fixed pricing applied to fractional coordination fees

**Spinny does:** Fixed non-negotiable pricing on cars.

**Nuanced translation:** Fixed pricing applies to LISTING prices (plot sale price) cleanly — copy this directly. But coordination fees for fractional deals SHOULD vary by complexity:
- Simple 3-buyer Kanakapura deal: ₹40-60K per buyer
- Complex 4-buyer deal with conversion needed + PTCL re-verification + non-resident buyer: ₹1L+ per buyer

Don't force fixed fees onto service SKUs where the underlying work varies significantly. Fixed pricing is a philosophical promise about the plot price, not a rule that applies to every SKU.

---

## 11. Customer cohort transactability

**Spinny assumption:** Used-car buyers transact every 3-5 years. Repeat-customer LTV is a real thing.

**Don't assume for plots:** Plot buyers buy once in a decade or lifetime. Repeat transactions per customer are rare. This changes:
- Marketing budget allocation (zero retention marketing for 3+ years; all acquisition)
- CRM strategy (relationships are for referrals, not re-sales)
- Loyalty programs (don't build one)
- Email/push cadence (don't retarget past buyers for new deals — they bought a plot; they're not buying another)

**What to do instead:** Design referral loops and case-study programs. Past buyers become evangelists, not repeat transactors.

---

## 12. Aggressive price-as-hook

**Cars24 does:** Price is primary differentiator. "Best price guaranteed" style messaging.

**Spinny deliberately doesn't:** Spinny leads with emotional trust ("Guaranteed Love"), not price.

**TruePlots should NOT lead with price:**
- Cheapest plot is typically a legal disaster (PTCL, unconverted, disputed)
- "TruePlots has the cheapest plots" is exactly the wrong promise
- The correct promise: "TruePlots has the CLEANEST plots at fair market price"

Resist every internal instinct to compete on price against 99acres/MagicBricks. You'll lose (they have volume inventory) and you'll attract the wrong buyer (legal-risk-tolerant).

Each of these has a legitimate-sounding argument. Each is wrong for TruePlots specifically.
